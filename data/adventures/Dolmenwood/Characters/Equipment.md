
## Armour

* Unarmored
	* 10+DEX def
* Leather Armor / Gambeson
	* 10 gp, 11+DEX def, Light
* Chainmail
	* 100 gp, 13+DEX def, Medium, iron
* Plate Armor
	* 200 gp, 15 def, Heavy, iron
* Buckler
	* 3 gp, +1 def, 1h, Small (3x), Slam (d4, Light Simple, Forceful/Finesse Melee)
* Shield
	* 10 gp, +2 def, 1h, 1 slot, Sacrifice, Slam (d4, Medium Simple, Forceful Melee)

Craftsmanship

* Bronze armor: 2x cost, -1 defense, non-iron
	* Skilled faerie smiths can make armor of bronze (or stranger materials) as good as iron.
* Mossling-craft: 5x cost, +1 defense, non-metallic
	* Bark ("leather") or Pinecone ("chainmail")
* Masterwork: 10x cost, +1 defense or -1 weight category
	* Armor only, not shields

Tags

* Weight
	* Light: 1 slot
	* Medium: 2 slots, +D swim, move silently, acrobatics
	* Heavy: 3 slots, no swim, +D move silently, acrobatics
* Sacrifice: shatters to absorb the damage from one hit

Encumbrance

* Small: you can carry up to 3 Small items without any load.  Beyond this, Small items bundle into stacks of 3, 10, or 20.



* Free Slots (considered Readied)
	* Carry 100 coins for free, then 1 slot per 100 coins
	* 3 free Small items (dagger, oil, thieves tools, 10 ammo)
* 3 Small items per slot, 10 bundled, 20 ammo
	* slot filled if any items present
* Armor = 0-3 slots
* Most other stuff is 1 slot

load
* max 10 equipped slots
	* quick access, in / ready for use
* max 16 stowed slots
	* 10 slots per large container (backpack, sack)
		* 1 backpack, 2 sacks (1 per hand)
		* containers are 0 slots when deployed, 1 slot when stowed
	* retrieving a stowed item: WIS check per round
* load
	* use the heaviest load between ready and stowed slots
	* modify the load stowed limits by CON
	* none (0-3 ready, 0-8 stowed): 40' move
	* light (4-5 ready, 9-12 stowed): 30' move
	* medium (6-7 ready, 13-14 stowed): 20' move
	* heavy (8-10 ready, 15-16 stowed): 10' move

penalty to climb, swim, sneak, jump
* worse of armor weight penalty or load (read load weight as armor weight)
* sneaking: -1 for medium, -2 for heavy
* CON checks for fatigue: -1 for medium, -2 for heavy (forced march, sleep rough, etc)
