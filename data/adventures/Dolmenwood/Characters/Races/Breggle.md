Burly and tough goat-folk.  Some are goat-headed, others have intermixed with humans and are more satyr-like: hairy, hooved, and horned, but human-faced.

Tough
* Start with +2 HP.  Roll HP each level with +A (1 extra die, keep the highest set).

Horns
* Deal +1 damage in melee combat.
* Your horns can serve as a weapon (melee, close, d4, finesse/forceful).

Beast of Burden
* +CON inventory slots (min 1, stacks w/ Hauler).

Iron Stomach
* +A to resist conditions caused by ingesting something.

Longhorn
* At 4th level and above, you are considered a Longhorn.  Gain Imperious Gaze.

Mad Revelry (DEX | WIS | CHA, Tier 2, Heavy Focus (1d4 rounds), Nearby listeners)

Imperious Gaze (STR | CON | CHA, Tier 2, Safe, Close mortal or beast you lock eyes with)
* Meet the gaze of a mortal or beast (P [1 + CL/2] vs R [TL+CHA-1])
	* Success: the target is filled with awe and trepidation toward you until it's next Long Rest.  It will not act aggressively toward you, but will defend itself.


Means of Introduction
* A Breggle or half-Breggle from from the surrounding areas of Dolmenwood who aren't totally unfamiliar with goat-folk.



Talents
* Imperious: cast Imperious Gaze with +A.
* Hard-Headed: increase bonus melee damage to +2.  Your horns deal d6.  When you ram someone or something, it has +D to resist being knocked down / broken through.

Bonus
* Add 2 points among STR and CON.
