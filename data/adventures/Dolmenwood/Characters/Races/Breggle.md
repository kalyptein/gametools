Burly and tough goat-folk.  Some are goat-headed, others have intermixed with humans and are more satyr-like: hairy, hooved, and horned, but human-faced.

## Traits

Tough
* Start with +2 HP.  When rolling HP each level, roll twice and use the higher total.

Horns
* Deal +1 damage in melee combat.
* Your horns can serve as a weapon (melee, close, d4, finesse/forceful).

Beast of Burden
* +CON inventory slots (min 1).

Iron Stomach
* +A to resist conditions caused by ingesting something.

Longhorn
* At 4th level and above, you are considered a Longhorn.  Gain Imperious Gaze.

Imperious Gaze
* STR | CON | CHA, Tier 2, Safe, A Close mortal or beast you lock eyes with
* Meeting the target's gaze, they are filled with awe and trepidation toward you until they next sleep.  They will not act aggressively toward you, but will defend themself.
	* P [LVL+2] vs R [Level+CHA]


## Advancement

Talents
* Really Impressive Horns
	* Cast Imperious Gaze with +A.
* Of the Line of Servants
	* You are resistant to frost and choose 1 Glamour.

Bonus
- Add 2 points among Strength and Constitution.
- Hard-Headed
    - +2 HP. Increase Horn bonus melee damage to +2. Your horns deal d6.


Means of Introduction
* A Breggle or half-Breggle from from the surrounding areas of Dolmenwood who aren't totally unfamiliar with goat-folk.
* A half-blood who is transforming
* A human who handled an ancient breggle artifact and was transformed
