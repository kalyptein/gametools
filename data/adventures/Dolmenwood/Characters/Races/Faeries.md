## Faerie Nature

These traits are common to faerie (grimalkin and elves).

Faerie Blood
* Vulnerable to Iron: iron weapons deal you +1 daamge.  You can handle iron, though it's unpleasant, but can't abide wearing iron armor.
* Ageless and immune to mundane disease.  Won't die of hunger or thirst, but becomes increasingly desperate and cruel.

Glamour
* Choose 1 Glamour to start with, and can learn more as talents.

Outside the Bounds of Heaven
* You cannot be a Cleric or Friar.
* Your prayers go unanswered.
	* e.g. can't invoke holy items, pray for free spells at a shrine, or use holy talismans.  You can benefit from holy spells, blessings, or relics with ongoing/passive benefits.
* You cannot be raised from the dead by holy magic.

Bound by Faerie Law
- Falsehood
	- You cannot speak direct falsehood, but may befuddle, omit, imply, pun, state hypotheticals, recite fiction, or remain silent.
- Hospitality
	- You are bound to your offer or acceptance of hospitality, to mortal, faerie, or other being.
		- Host and guest(s) may not harm, enchant, or rob each other, and are bound by any restrictions or bargains imposed when hospitality is offered and accepted.
		- The host must offer food, drink, and shelter if able, though it may be poor and grudging.
		- Guests and hosts are responsible for the actions of their subordinates and property.
		- A host may revoke hospitality.  Guests must be allowed to leave peaceably.
		- Violators are marked for punishment by the Realms and Rulers of Faerie.
* Gifts
	* If you give someone a gift and don't receive some recompense or promise in return, the receiver becomes Indebted to you. If you receive a gift and don't offer some recompense or promise in return, you become Indebted to the giver.
	    - If a promise is reneged upon, the promise-breaker is thrice-Indebted.
	- Indebted: vulnerable to the magic and social influence of the one they owe, and the one they owe is Resistant to their magic and social influence. This applies the next time it is attempted, and for a day thereafter.
	    - This law is longer-lasting and more powerful for great Lords and Ladies of Faerie than for common fey.
* The Teind
	* Every 7 years, the faerie as a whole must pay the Teind to Hell of 7 maids and 7 youths.
	- This is mostly a matter for the great Lords and Ladies to sort out, though mortals who fall under their power (great debts, oaths of fealty, etc) might be offered up.
