## Faerie Nature

Faerie Law
- fey cannot speak direct falsehood
	- they can befuddle, omit, imply, pun, state things as examples, recite fiction, or remain silent (and often do)
- if a fey gives a promise of hospitality, they are bound to it
	- they can revoke it, and give the guest leave to depart
	- hospitality at a campsite is auto-revoked when camp is broken
	- if the guest breaks guest-law (attacking, stealing from, disobeying clear restrictions placed upon them), hospitality is immediately revoked
		- a host may try to trick the guest into breaking guest-law
	- the host may not attack, place enchantments, steal from, or deny food/drink/shelter (though they might be poor and grudging) 
	- a guest's subordinates are their responsibility, a breach by one is a breach by the guest
	- a host's subordinates are their responsibility, and are also bound by their declaration
- they fey as a whole must pay the Teind to Hell every 7 years
	- 7 maids and 7 youths
	- this is mostly a matter for the great Lords and Ladies to sort out, though mortals who fall under their power (great debts, oaths of fealty, etc) might be offered up
- demi-fey are not bound by these laws, though many may have cultural traditions that weigh them heavily

Vulnerable to Cold Iron
* applies to iron/steel; specially crafted "cold iron" is above and beyond this
- +1 damage per die from iron
- can handle iron (it's unpleasant), but can't wear iron/steel armor (too itchy)
	- can use bronze, etc armor
	- made by mortal smiths, it costs as much, and has -1 defense
	- faerie and demi-fey smiths can make non-iron armor with equal defense

Glamour
* start w/ 1 glamour
* can learn more as feats?

Immortality
* ageless
* immune to mundane disease
* don't die or hunger or thirst, but become desperate and cruel

Magic Resistance
* +2 to saves against magic

Outside the Bounds of Heaven
* Cannot be Cleric or Friars.
* Cannot receive benefit from prayer (invoking holy items, praying for a free spell in a shrine, using holy talismans, being anointed with holy oil/water, etc).
	* Can use holy items with ongoing/passive benefits.
* Cannot be raised from the dead by holy magic.
