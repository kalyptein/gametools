## Common Skills

* Characters begin with TN 6 in all common skills.

Search
* base TN 6
* for finding secret compartments, doors, and other subtle features

Listen
* base TN 6
* used when trying to hear subtle things (quietly lurking monsters, eavesdropping on hushed whispers)
	* some creatures are wholly silent (many undead, constructs)
* for less subtle sounds, a Wisdom check can be used, with a bonus of (6-Listen)
* 
* ??? can be used to pick up extra rumors in town?

Survival
* base TN 6
* Used in a variety of situations
	* Finding Food: foraging (finding plants, edible or alchemical), hunting (finding quarry), fishing (catching fish)
* Fire-starting in bad conditions
* Shelter-finding

Sneak
* base TN 5
* Used for surprise checks against a foe when you're being quiet enough for it to be a possibility.
* Used to move unobserved when near a listener.



## Class Skills

Sheer Climb
* for sheer climbing
* add (6-Climb) as a bonus to rough climbing stat checks
A successful check allows a thief to climb vertical or very steep surfaces with only minimal handholds. The thief does not require any special climbing equipment.
Easier circumstances: Thieves make easier climbs without a roll (e.g. see Climbing, p150).
Long climbs: For climbs of over 100′, a Climb Wall Check is required for each stretch of up to 100′. For example, a climb of 150′ requires two checks.
Failure: The thief is unable to find suitable handholds and makes no progress with the climb.

Natural 1: On a roll of natural 1, the thief must Save Versus Doom or fall from the half way point, suffering 1d6 damage per 10′ of the fall.
Retrying: The thief may retry failed attempts as often as they wish, each subsequent attempt requiring 1 Turn.

Disable Mechanism
A successful check allows a thief to disarm complex, clockwork-like trap mechanisms hidden in a lock, lid, door handle, or similar. Thieves’ tools are required for disarming mechanism traps (see Adventuring Gear, p116).
Time: Each attempt to disarm a trap requires 1 Turn. 
Retrying: The thief may retry failed attempts as often as they wish, each attempt requiring an additional 1 Turn. 
Natural 1: On a roll of natural 1, the thief must Save Versus Doom or accidentally spring the trap.

Pick Lock
A successful check allows a thief to open a lock without the key. Thieves’ tools are required for picking locks (see Adventuring Gear, p116).
Time: Each attempt to pick a lock requires 1 Turn. 
Retrying: The thief may retry failed attempts as often as they wish, each attempt requiring an additional 1 Turn. 
Difficult locks: The Referee may rule that certain locks are more difficult. Advanced locks incur a penalty to the Pick Lock Check or only allow a fixed number of attempts, after which the thief is stymied and may only try again after gaining a Level.

Detect Magic

Legerdemain
A successful check allows a thief to pilfer a small item in the possession of another creature or perform a trick of sleight of hand, such as palming a small object or slipping a poison into a drink.
Difficulty: –1 penalty per three Levels of the victim or observer (e.g. a Level 6 target incurs a –2 penalty).
Natural 1: On a roll of natural 1, the thief must Save Versus Doom to avoid being noticed. The Referee determines the victim’s reaction.

Lore

Decipher
A successful check allows a thief to understand the gist of a non-magical text in a language they do not speak, unravel a cypher, or identify cryptically labelled landmarks on a map.
Retrying: The thief may only attempt to read the same document again after gaining a Level.

- allows bards and thieves to try to use arcane scrolls and potentially other magic-word-activated arcane items
	- first time you try to use the item, roll Decipher.
		- success activates it (and allows future use, if reusable, but fresh roll even for same scroll type)
		- failure means it doesn't work, can try again next level
		- nat 1: misfire; expend the scroll or a charge/use of item.  save vs doom; fail and it misfires (wrong target, etc), success and it just fizzles
- can use Decipher to try to forge things (writing, but with skill or skilled assistance, objects, seals, etc)

Tracking
* An attempt at tracking take 1 Turn and covers a 30' x 30' area.
	* If successful, you find any tracks.  You know the type and rough number of creatures that made the tracks.  You may follow them until conditions worsen, requiring a new check to continue.
	* If you fail, you can't retry in the same location.
* You can attempt to search in urban, indoor, or subterranean environments, but it is often heavily penalized (hard flooring, passage of many others, etc).
* Modifiers
	* soft ground +1
	* hard ground –1
	* tracks of a group of 10+ creatures +1
	* active attempts to cover tracks (dragging brush, walking carefully, crossing water, etc) –2
	* per day since the tracks were made –1
	* per hour of rain or covering snow –1
	* poor lighting –1

Alertness
* If the party is surprised, this you may roll Alertness.  If successful, you are not surprised (act normally, no penalties, etc).

Stealth
* roll to maintain concealment when hidden by only light obscurement (shadows, slight clutter)
	* if you have partial cover, make the X-in-6 check first; if it fails, roll stealth
* if a Sneak check fails, roll Stealth.  If successful you remain undetected (if alone, you're fully undetected, if with others who aren't stealthy, you are hidden at the start of the encounter).

Wayfinding
* If the party becomes lost, the GM should roll Wayfinding for characters that have it.  If successful, they find their path again.
