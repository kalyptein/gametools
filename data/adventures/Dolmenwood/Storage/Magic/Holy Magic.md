#### Command

- Prayer name: St Ingrid’s Admonition
	- Miracle: as written
- Duration: 1 Round
- Range: 10′
- The caster utters a command charged with holy power. A selected creature within range is magically compelled to obey for 1 Round.
	- Command: The command is limited to a single word (e.g. flee, back, stop, surrender, leave, etc.).  Commands that directly harm the subject or that enact evil or abusive deeds cause the spell to fail.
	- The target must have a mind (not a slime, construct, mindless undead, etc) and speak the language in which the command is uttered for it to take effect.
	- Saving Throw: creatures Level 6+ or with a Wisdom bonus to saves may make a save vs Hold to negate.  Otherwise there is no save.
#### Augury

- Prayer name: St Dougan’s Cup
	- Miracles: Dougan was a pious young squire traveling in search of worthy errantry.  As he approached an inn for the night, he beheld a cup of blood above its door.  Heeding the warning and sleeping in a humble hayloft, he escaped robbery and murder at the hands of the wicked innkeeper.  As he passed a cave, he beheld a brimming cup of wine, and within found an enchanted sword.  Finally, at a fork in the road, he beheld both cups on the left path, leading him to a starving village whose orchards had been seized by a terrible ogre.  With the magic sword, he slew it, freeing the people and winning his spurs as a knight.  In his old age, he gave up to the sword to become a monk, offering guidance to seekers.
- Duration: 1 round
- Whispering a prayer for insight into a proposed course of action ("taking the left path", "opening the chest", "talking with the witch"), the caster stands oblivious for a Round, granted a brief vision of two chalices. The righthand fills with wine, and the lefthand with blood, indicating the degrees of weal and woe that the topic is likely to bring to the caster within a span of time up to a day.
	- The amounts are roughly "empty", "a splash", "a draught", and "brimming".
	- Weal can include gaining wealth, displaying virtue and honor, learning information, achieving goals, escaping trouble, making a new ally, etc.
	- Woe can include losing valuables, suffering harm, facing danger, temptation to sin, going astray, being betrayed or tricked, etc.
	- Outcomes are judged on the caster's desires and the saint's righteous intent for them.
		- The measures can indicate a rough ratio, but also take into account likelihood of outcomes.  Asking about a terrible dragon likely to destroy the caster would give a brimming cup of blood and a meagre splash of wine.  Even though the dragon has a grand hoard to claim, there is little chance of doing so; the chance of woe vastly outweighs weal.  If the caster was more equal to the danger, both wine and blood might be brimming.
	- If the topic is somehow invalid, the vision of the chalices begins, then wavers and fades, indicating an incorrect assumption.
- Span of Time: the guidance is based on the most useful span of time, up to a day.  The DM can discuss with the player if they want to limit it to a shorter span for greater clarity.
	- The caster might be considering opening a door.  Over the course of the day, this might lead to many perils, but they want to know if opening the door will immediately trigger a trap.  They request guidance limited to the immediate future.

#### Detect Evil

- Prayer name: St Whittery’s Vision
	- Miracle: as written
* Duration: 6 Turns
* Range: 120′
* The caster perceives a faint halo of wicked, grinning spirits flickering around objects and places infused with evil essence, or under an evil enchantment, and beings with evil nature or intentions.  The nature of the halo grants insight into the nature and quality of the evil.
	* Beings of evil nature include undead and demons.  Priests, warlocks, witches and similar folk who traffic with such beings also carry their nature, if they are of significant power.
	* You can sense the presence of invisible and ethereal evil beings within range, but not directly perceive or locate them.
	* Evil enchantments are those that inflict prolonged suffering (e.g. a cruel geas or curse, a spell of torment), draw upon or summon evil beings, or violate natural or divine law (e.g. necromancy).  They might have potentially moral uses, but they detect as evil.
	* Evil intention includes an imminent intent to kill, rob, assault, deceive, coerce, despoil, desecrate, ruin, enthrall, spy upon, etc without any rationale beyond greed, cruelty, malice, callous expedience, or other self-serving motives.
	* The presence of evil intent can be sensed, but the exact intent isn't made known.
* Ritual
	* Cast as a 1 Turn ritual, this enchants a person or object (a 5' radius moving with them/it) or an area (up to nine 10' cubes, which need not be contiguous) for 1 day.
	* Those who enter the area are scrutinized by Detect Evil, and if judged evil, an angelic horn sounds.
		* It can be heard by the caster up to 10 miles away.  Those the caster would wish to be warned (doesn't need to be specified in advance) can hear it from up to 500' away, while others hear nothing.  The sleepers who hear it wake instantly and without grogginess.

#### Detect Magic

- Prayer name: Wisdom of St Thorm
	- Miracle: as written
- Duration: 6 Turns
- Range: 60′
- Enchanted objects, areas, or creatures in the caster's view and within range are wreathed in shimmering colours for all to see. Both permanent and temporary enchantments are revealed.
	- The colours and flux reveal the magnitude, form, location, area, rough duration, and aspects of the magic (see magical analysis rules) to one knowledgeable in reading them.
		- It takes a round or more to inspect the details of an individual enchantment, depending on complexity and subtlety.
	- The aspects of magic readily reveal if it is arcane, holy, fey, demonic, etc.
	- It does not reveal enchantments of deception or concealment (e.g. illusions and invisibility).
- At will, the caster can evoke or suppress the effect, or limit it to a specific area, throughout the duration of the spell.

#### Ward Against Frost and Flame

- Prayer name: St Abthius’ Rebuke
	- The Miracle of St Abthius, Ever-Lustrous: As a young acolyte, Abthius was in a village square, begging alms.  Hearing a commotion, he found a baker's apprentice had fallen into the oven and climbed in to rescue him, emerging untouched by the flames.  Much later as a bishop, Abthius came to Dolmenwood at the command of King Thaldred II of Brackenwold. The Cold Prince had newly returned his frigid gaze to the mortal world, and Abthius—renowned for his fiery sermons—served as ambassador of the King. Gaining an audience with the fairy court, the saint proceeded to preach the virtues of the One True God and the host of angels. The fairies responded only with mocking laughter, and their liege pointed his crystal wand at the saint, casting a spell of ice about him. The saint’s radiant beauty melted the fey frost, and he turned his back on the Prince, damning the fairy to be vaporized in the flames of perdition. 
- Range: 30′
- Choose frost or flame upon casting.
	- Frost: a soothing warmth rebukes the malicious effects of cold and frost.
		- Area: all allies within range.
		- Duration: 6 Turns
		- Mundane cold: Subjects are untroubled by non-magical freezing temperatures.  They will not lose their footing on ice.
		- Save bonus: Subjects gain a +2 bonus to Saving Throws versus cold-based effects (e.g. magic or breath attacks). 
		- Cold-based damage: Reduce cold damage by 1 per damage die rolled. (For example, 4d6 damage is reduced by 4.)
	- Flame: an aroma of fresh baked bread fills the air, born on a cool breeze.
		- Area: A single creature or object in range.
		- Duration: 2 Turns
		- Normal heat: The subject is unharmed by non-magical heat, fire, smoke, and ash.
		- Save bonus: The subject gains a +2 bonus to Saving Throws versus fire-based effects (e.g. magic or breath attacks). 
		- Fire-based damage: Reduce fire damage by 1 per damage die rolled. (For example, 4d6 damage is reduced by 4.)
- Ritual Casting
	- If an hour is spent in prayer, this spell can be cast upon a tent or dwelling.  It protects the structure for 1 day.
		- Frost: the interior is snug and draught-free, the roof won't collapse from snow buildup, etc.
		- Flame: the interior is pleasantly cool, the thatch won't catch flame, the interior remains free from smoke, etc.

#### Light

- Duration: 12 Turns
- Range: 120′
- A bobbing wisp of light floats from the caster’s palm and manifests one of the following effects:

#### Mantle of Protection

- Prayer name: St Benester’s Word
	- Miracle: as written
- Duration: 12 Turns
- Range: The caster or a creature touched
- Invoking the name of an archangel, the subject is divinely warded.
	- Grants +1 to defense and saving throws.
	- Unearthly creatures (undead, demons, faerie, elementals, magically summoned or created beings, etc) cannot touch or attack the subject in melee, but can attack at range or use powers.
		- If the subject attacks an unearthly creature in melee, this extra protection is lost.
	- The subject's spirit is completely protected against possession, theft, etc.
		- If they are energy drained, the mantle flares in a burst of golden light, ending it and negating the drain.

#### Clarity

- Prayer name: St Jorrael’s Counsel
	- Miracle: as written
- Duration: 6 Turns
- Range: 30'
- The emboldening words of this prayer reverberate in the subject’s mind, granting calm and clarity.  It soothes either moderate emotions in all within range, or strong emotions in 1 creature per 3 Levels of the caster.
	- Moderate emotions: unfounded mistrust or anger, uneasiness, temptation, disorganization, tipsiness, etc.  A mistrustful mob could be made to listen to the caster's words, drunken revelry could be quelled, or aggressive animals could be soothed.

The spell cannot calm the inherent nature of malign beings (the hunger or vengefulness of the undead, the cruelty of demons, etc).
