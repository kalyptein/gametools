## Cosmology

- the doctrine of the Church is not necessarily the cosmic truth of the divine.
	- there is a benevolent force
	- some people can wield its power
	- some people (saints) are bridges to it and their bones/items/etc are relics that hold its power.
	- exact rules about behavior, gender, who can be a priest, etc may not be as the Church says.
- priests should roll their own theology, what they believe, though they may bite their tongues to avoid being declared a heretic (the Pluritines are a bit flexible, but not completely without fixed doctrine).

## Invoke Divine Authority

- displaying it proves you are a genuine cleric in the eyes of faithful or magical beings (faerie, demons, other priests, etc).
	- it may convince divine entities or powers to heed your requests (a divine ward or guardian to let you pass, etc).  it doesn't compel them, so it's up to the GM and perhaps a turning roll.
- Turn Undead
	- turn undead works because the undead are mortal-souled beings subject to divine judgement (or lesser creatures subject to the authority of man); it doesn't work on never-mortal beings (faerie, wood gods, etc) even with a "deathly aspect" to them, and doesn't work on demons (need to use spells to deal with them, not just turning)
	- once your turning has failed to destroy a given undead, it can't do more than make them flee until 24 hours have passed

## Casting

* divine miracles are spontaneously cast from your spell list; there is no prep
- after a night's sleep, you can pray in peace for an hour and regain all your spell slots
- must be able to speak, don't need hands or implements
- if you bear a relic of a saint, if you can already cast their miracle, you get a free tier of upcasting.

### Spell List

- Each Saint is known for their signature miracle.
- A holy caster must attune to a saint to gain permanent access to their signature miracle by doing one of the following:
	- Spend a vigil in prayer at their shrine (which must be in good, sanctified order).
	- Retrieve one of their relics and restore it to church hands to serve the faithful.
	- Walk in the saint's footsteps by emulating their signature trial or suffering (survive being shipwrecked, etc).
	- Face serious hardship doing a worthy service (save lives, preach to foes of the faith, slay a monster, undertake a quest, etc) for someone associated with the saint (keepers of their shrine, people or places they are a patron of, etc).
		- The hardship should be at least relative to someone who could perform the miracle (e.g. Raise Dead requires 9th level, so it would be a deed worthy of a 9th level hero).
- A holy caster can gain temporary access to a miracle while:
	- On holy ground during the saint's holy day.
	- Brandishing a relic of the saint while casting.
