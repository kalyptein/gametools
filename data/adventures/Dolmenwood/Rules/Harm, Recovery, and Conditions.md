* Decay (acid, entropy, necrotic)
* Health (poison, disease, metabolic disruption)
* Spirit (psychic, eldritch)

### Resistance & Vulnerability

Resistance to X [Y]
* Against X effects with Potency, take +2 Resist.
* Against X effects with a Resist roll, take +A.
* If Y is listed, reduce X damage taken by Y.

Vulnerability to X [Y]
* Against X effects with Potency, take -2 Resist.
* Against X effects with a Resist roll, take +D.
* If Y is listed, increase X damage taken by Y, up to double the initial amount.









### Mortality

* If you take damage and can't soak it with HP:
	* Roll CON DC 12 to avoid taking a Wound.  On success, gain 1 Fatigue instead.
	* Roll CON DC 15 to avoid collapsing.
		* You are longer collapsed once you have at least 1 HP.
	* For every full 10 unsoaked damage taken, gain a Deathmark.
		* If already unstable, gain +1 Deathmark.
	* You become unstable.
		* You are longer unstable once you have at least 1 HP, or are stabilized.
* While unstable:
	* If newly unstable, the GM secretly rolls 1d4+CON for your Death Count.
	* At the end of your turn, roll a d20.
		* 20+: regain 1 Guard.
		* 15-19: no change
		* 14-: gain a Deathmark.  If your Deathmarks > Death Count, you die.
* First Aid
	* Roll INT (Medicine) DC 15 to stabilize a subject with tools (a healer's kit, torn strips of clothing as bandages, etc).  Without tools, or if treating yourself, take +D.

## Recovery
---
Rests

* Type
	* Quick: 1 minute
	* Brief: 10 minutes
		* Collapsed but stable characters regain 1 Guard.
	* Short: 1 hour, need to have rations and water, but consume a negligible amount.
		* If you consume an extra ration, fully heal your Guard.
	* Long: 8 hours, consume a ration and water, get some sleep.
		* There is time included for camp tasks (cooking, raising tents, standing watch).
		* Full heal your Guard and remove 2 Deathmarks.
			* If tended by someone trained in Medicine, clear +1 Deathmark.
		* If you consume an extra ration, clear 1 Deathmark and either heal 1dHD+CON Grit or remove 1 Fatigue.
	* Respite: 24 hours of rest and relaxation in civilized comfort.
		* Gain the benefits of a Long Rest as if consuming an extra ration.  Heal an extra 1dHD Grit and 1 Fatigue.
* Notes
	* You can only eat 1 extra ration per Long Rest.
	* Grit, Fatigue, and stat damage don't otherwise recover in the field.
* Poor Resting Conditions (Short and Long Rests)
	* Poor sleep (from actual interruption or the stress of constant vigilance or fear), oppressive heat or cold, discomfort, filth, accursed / unholy vibes, hunger, thirst, etc.
	* For each condition, roll CON (default DC 12) or gain reduced / no benefit from resting.
		* Refresh only 1d4 spells.  Heal only half your max Guard.
		* Don't benefit from eating an extra ration.
			* No Grit, no stat damage healed for a Long Rest
			* No Guard regained for a Short Rest, but the benefit can be taken later if better conditions are found.
	* You can't take a Respite if any Poor Conditions apply.

Healing Effects

* Most magical healing can only restore Guard.
* Consumables (healing potions, herbs, etc) can heal Grit and an equal amount of Guard (excess Grit also goes into Guard).

## Hazards
---
Falling
* Take 1d6 damage per 10' fallen, with 6's exploding into d6-1 (further exploding on 6's).
	* Each size below Medium, a creature reduces the die size used by 1 step (d4 for Small, d2 for Tiny).  Each size above Medium, increase the dice size 1 step (d8 for Large, d10 for Huge, d12 for Gargantuan, and d20 for Colossal).
* You can intentionally drop 10' safely.
* If you are sliding on a slope, able to grasp at handholds, or landing on a somewhat forgiving surface, you can resist with DEX to break your fall and take half damage.

Suffocation / Drowning
* After a Brief Rest, heal 75% of HP lost to suffocation.  The rest becomes normal damage.

Exertion
* Hard Travel
	* Each day per condition, roll CON DC 15 or gain 2 Exhaustion, or only 1 on success.
		* Forced Marching, travel under punishing weather (downpour, heat, cold, etc), or over very difficult terrain (deep bogs, jagged hills, etc).
* Traveling under a Heavy Load
	* Each day of overland travel adds 1 Exhaustion.
* Hard Labor
	* A span of hard labor (digging, hauling, demolishing, climbing, etc) may impose points of Exhaustion, perhaps with a CON roll to resist / reduce it.
	* Torture, squalid imprisonment, or other extreme privation may be handled similarly.

Hunger
* Every day without eating at least 1 ration, roll CON DC 15 or gain 1 Hunger.
* Every day you eat an extra ration, in addition to the recovery benefits, also remove 1 Hunger.

Thirst
* Every day without drinking at least 1 measure, gain 5 Thirst.
* Every day you have access to plentiful drink, remove 5 Thirst.

Sleep Deprivation
* Every day without sleep, gain (days without sleep) Sleep Deprivation (so 1, 2, 3, 4, etc).
* Every day of normal sleep, remove 1 Sleep Deprivation.  Every day of pure rest, remove 5 Sleep Deprivation.

Extreme Environmental Temperature
* Travel in sufficiently extreme heat or cold without adequate protection periodically deals frost or fire damage.
	* Intervals are usually once per 4 hours, 1 hour, or 10 minutes.
	* This damage cannot be healed without first warming up / cooling off.
