## Round Order

* alternating activation


* readied and delayed actions



## Actions

main
* attack
	* strike (full and/or half)
	* parry (trade a full strike for +A defense vs one attack you can parry)
* dash: move Near
* charge: move twice Near and attack, -2 defense, +2 damage at end of straight line move
* dodge/brace: +A to defense rolls and STR and DEX resists, can't attack
* full offense: 
* cast
* aim: +A to next shot or throw
* deed: force, lock, inflict
* 3 minor actions
* use item
* retrieve stowed item
* task
* aid another

move
* advance: move Near
* shift: shift Close
* stand
* 1 minor action

minor
* retrieve/pocket readied item, draw/sheathe weapon
* manipulate (door, lever, etc)
* hand-off, pick-up
* minor cast

free
* on-turn
	* drop item
	* fall prone
* off-turn (doesn't interrupt)
	* talk

reaction
* readied action
* AoO
	* when provoked; if you "hit", the action is disrupted
	* except for provoking movement: a hit halts movement and also deals damage

variable
* reload (main, move, minor)






* shield wall: +1 defense
* shield sundering
* dual wield
	* roll damage w/ both weapons, use the higher result OR +1 defense this round


X Resistance
* Against X, you gain whichever is most appropriate from: +A to resistance rolls, half damage, or +2 Resist.


decisions
* can move and cast
* provoke w/ casting, skill, ranged, etc when threatened
	* a "hit" doesn't deal damage but negates the action
* provoke by moving
	* a hit deals damage and halts / zeroes out movement


## Harm

Death
* 0 hp
* -1 hp or lower
	* fall unconscious
	* death in 1d4+CON rounds (end of round), rolled secretly
		* every 5 damage (or fraction thereof) taken removes 1 round
	* any healing stabilizes
	* stabilize with an INT DC 15 check and tools (healer's kit, torn up clothing, etc)
	* on your turn, roll d20, stabilizing at 0 hp on a 20+
* unconsciousness
	* an enemy can coup de grace and kill an unconscious foe as an action as long as they are not Threatened.




recovery idea
* there are slot-filling "items"
	* Fatigue
		* also various things like Thirst, Hunger, Sleep Dep, etc
	* Flesh Wound
	* Serious Wound (typed)

* if you have wounds and fatigue = your carrying capacity, you collapse, overcome
	* can't push fatigue / wounds into overflow (though you can carry gear in overflow as you get wearier from fatigue / wounds)

* 
* if you suffer a crit, CON DC 12 to avoid a short-term serious wound (leaves a flesh wound, but the effect fades outside combat; maybe a Shake Off (10 min) roll, CON DC 15 each roll to end it)?

*
