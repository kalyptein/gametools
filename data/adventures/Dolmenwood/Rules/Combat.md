## Structure of Combat
---
### Surprise

* Surprised combatants have a -20 modifier to initiative, and are Dazed until the end of their first turn.  They can't choose to flee during the Intentions Phase this round.
* Usually results from a successful ambush / sneak attack, or an unprepared target (drunk, distracted, etc).
	* More extreme penalties can be applied for more extreme forms of surprise, a masterful ambush or completely unexpected betrayals could leave the victims completely Stunned the first round, etc.

### "Speed Sandwich" Initiative

* NPCs have fixed Init values (elites and solos with multiple actions have multiple numbers).
* PCs roll d10 + DEX + Modifiers
	* Size
		* Tiny (+2), Large (-2), Huge (-4)
	* Weapon
		* Small (+2), Large (-2)
	* Armor
		* Heavy (-2)
	* Encumbrance
		* Heavy (-4)
	* Circumstances (-4 to +4)
* All PCs who beat the NPC value (PCs win ties) can intermingle their actions freely.
	* Actions can be readied to interrupt on their trigger, or delayed and used later in the round.
	* A PC's turn begins as soon as they have the ability to act.
	* A PCs turn ends as soon as they have used all actions or chosen to ready or delay them.
		* If they act later in the round, they don't repeat beginning-of or end-of turn effects.

### Turn Order

* Intentions Phase
	* Flee
		* The side chooses to withdraw, which may be automatically successful, require skill checks to extricate, and/or begin a chase.
	* Parley
		* One side attempts to open a dialog.  If no one on either side vetoes, a lull in battle happens to allow parleying.  Any member of either side can end the lull and proceed with battle.
	* Battle
		* Proceed with the next round of battle.
* Battle Phase
	* Beginning of Round
		* PCs roll initiative.
	* Turns
		* Start
			* Start of turn effects.
			* Roll spell Focus checks.
			* Gains 1 main, 1 move, and 1 minor action.
		* Actions (any order, may be interleaved with allied actions)
		* End
			* End of turn effects.
	* End of Round
		* Resolve end of round effects.
		* Unused delayed actions expire.
		* Begin next Intentions Phase

### Morale

* Common Triggers
	* Taken half casaualties / individuals dropping to half HP
	* Loss of leader
	* For more cowardly types, the first few casualties
	* Display of overwhelming force, savagery, or invicibility
	* Caught in an ambush
* Roll d20 vs DC based on enemy's training / psychology.
	* Fearless: never breaks (golem, zombies, berserkers)
	* Stalwart: DC 4
	* Brave: DC 7
	* Steady: DC 10
	* Shaky: DC 13
	* Cowardly: DC 17
	* Bolstered: roll w/ +A
		* Fighting for convictions, terrified of leader or consequences of failure, nowhere to run, strongly outnumber their foes, promise of great reward or fame
	* Weakened: roll w/ +D
		* Unprepared, battle's goals / purpose are weak or uncertain, not currently seeking battle (scouting, etc), greatly outnumbered, weak convictions, have endured privation
* On failure, the side seeks to retreat in accordance with their training, circumstances, psychology, etc.
	* e.g. fighting withdrawal, hasty retreat, rout, offer surrender or ransom, tripping each other to be first to escape, etc.
	* A second failure probably means a rout, even for well trained enemies.
	* Some monsters, instead of fleeing, may go berserk or display other extreme behaviors.

## Actions
---
### Main Actions

* Attack
	* Make an attack (or more, if allowed) during your turn.
		* (optional) Reckless: gain +A to one attack roll.  You have +D to defense until the start of your next turn.
		* (optional) Perform Deed: spend an attack to Force, Grapple, or Inflict Condition instead of a normal attack.
	* Missile weapon attacks Provoke (thrown attacks do not).
* Charge
	* Take -2 defense until the start of your next turn, and move Near.
	* You may  make a melee attack.
		* If you move roughly straight and over clear terrain before the attack, roll damage with +A.
		* If you have multiple attacks, you may spend them as normal throughout the round, but only one can gain the damage boost.
		* (optional) Reckless: as for Attack.
* Dash
	* Take a Move Action, or take -2 defense until the start of your next turn and move up to 2x Near throughout the round.
* Defend
	* Gain Resistance to weapon attacks and physical, single-target DEX effects.
		* If using a Shield, this works against physical area effects and STR effects as well.
	* You don't Threaten.
* Aim
	* Gain +A to your next shot or throw before the end of your next turn.  Moving, being attacked, or being targeted with an effect spoils your aim.
	* Aiming Provokes.
* Aid Attack
	* Grant 1 allied attack this round +2 attack and +1 damage (stacks up to 4x from multiple Aid Attack actions).
* Use Item
	* Use an item in hand (drink a potion, wave a wand, etc).
	* Most item use Provokes.
* Retrieve Stowed Item
	* Make a DEX DC 15 check to pull out a Stowed item.  If you repeat this action after failing the prior round, take +A.  If you dump out your pack and paw through them, take +A.
* Task
	* Perform an action (pick a lock, operate a machine, provide medical aid, etc).
	* Most tasks Provoke.
* Minor
	* Take 3 minor actions
* Coup de Grace
	* You kill a helpless target in melee range.  You cannot do this if you are Threatened.

### Move Actions

* Advance
	* Move up to Near throughout the round, interspersed with other actions.
* Shift
	* Shift Close.  Shifting does not Provoke.  You can't Shift while Prone.
* Stand
	* Rise from Prone.
* Minor
	* Take 1 minor action.

### Minor Actions

* Retrieve Readied Item
	* Bring a Readied item to hand or put an item in hand away in a Readied slot (e.g. draw / sheath a weapon)
* Manipulate
	* Pull a lever, open a door, pick up an unguarded item, etc.
* Hand-off
	* Give an item in hand to a willing target or take an item from them (only one of you has to spend the action)

### Free Actions

* Drop Held Item
* Fall Prone

### Reactions

* Readied Action
	* Invest available actions, attacks, and/or movement, and specify the trigger.
	* Take a previously readied action when the stated trigger occurs.  This interrupts the triggering event.
* Countermagic
	* Invest an available action (as per Ready Action) with the trigger "a perceived target imposes an effect / casts a spell".
	* If triggered, you can try to counter the impending spell with one of your own (the invested action must be sufficient to cast the spell).  See Arcane Magic for details.
* Attack of Opportunity
	* When a target you Threaten Provokes, make an attack against them.  If you hit, their action is disrupted.
		* If the action was a move, they halt and can't move further until the start of their next turn, and take normal attack damage.
	* Starting a move (that isn't a Shift) from a Threatened position to another Threatened position Provokes.
* Parry
	* Spend 1 available attack to ready a parry.
	* As a reaction, spend the parry to make an opposed attack roll against an attack that is about to hit you or a target in reach (after its outcome is rolled).
		* Melee weapons can parry melee and thrown attacks, but not missiles.  Shields can parry any weapon.  Some attacks may be too massive to parry (giants swinging trees, dragon tail lashes, charging behemoths, etc).
			* Parrying with a missile weapon probably damages it.
		* You can only parry a given attack once, even if you have multiple parries readied.

Variable Actions

* Cast (main | minor | free | reaction)
	* Cast a spell.  Defaults to a Main action.
	* Main action casting Provokes.
* Reload (main | move | minor)
	* Reload a weapon or item.  Main action reloading Provokes.

## Attacking
---
### Critical Hits

* Occurs on a natural 20 or success against the Defense+10 (e.g. a 25+ vs defense 15).
* Inflict maximum damage to the outmost layer of HP (Guard -> Grit), and 1d to the next layer inward (if it's empty, apply it to the outermost layer as well).

### Multi-Attacks

* Some abilities give additional attacks, sometimes in half steps (1x, 1.5x, 2x, etc).
* A "half attack" is rolled normally, but deals half damage, rounded up.
	* Half attacks can't be used to Perform Deeds.
* Multiple attacks can be spread them throughout the round, moving or taking other actions in between.

### Melee Attacks

* Sneak Attack
	* An attack against an alert and prepared target, but from an unknown and unexpected position, gains +A.
* Flanking
	* When multiple melee Threatening combatants flank or encircle a target, they gain +A to attack it.
		* An attacker who is at risk of being flanked themselves cannot participate in flanking (no flanking conga lines).
		* For each size above Medium, a target requires 1 extra enemy to achieve Flanking against it (e.g. a Large ogre requires 3 attackers to flank it, a Huge dragon requires 4).
* Elevation
	* Melee attacks against an elevated target (mounted, standing on significantly higher ground) from combatants below them take +D unless they are using Large or Reach weapons.

* Dual Wielding
	* With a DEX of at least +1, while fighting with a 1h weapon in each hand (offhand weapon must be Small, includes bucklers) or with a 2h Twin weapon, you are dual wielding.
	* Choose either the offensive or defensive benefit.  You can change this once each round during your turn.
		* Defensive: +1 defense and parry against melee attacks
		* Offensive: when you make a melee attack, roll the damage die for both weapons (or the twin weapon base and offhand damage) and use the higher roll.
* Hold at Bay
	* With a Reach weapon, targets with restricted mobility Provoke when moving closer with a Shift.  A hit disrupts their movement, but doesn't deal damage.
		* Restricted Mobility: approaching down a passageway, Slowed, difficult terrain
* Set Against Charge
	* Readied attacks and/or Attacks of Opportunity with piercing weapons deal +1d damage against a target charging, dashing, jumping, or otherwise moving rapidly toward you.

### Ranged Attacks

* Range
	* Normal Range: attacks within the listed range are made normally.
		* Ranged attacks take +D against at Tiny targets.
	* Long Range: attacks up to double the listed range take +D.
		* Tiny targets can't be hit.  Huge or larger targets (or similar-sized groups of smaller targets) have no penalty to be hit.
* Visibility
	* In Dim Light or Light Obscuration, take +D with ranged attacks at Normal Range, and they cannot be made at Long Range.  Treat both conditions at once as Darkness.
	* In Darkness or Heavy Obscuration, ranged attacks are impossible.
* Ranged Attacks Into Melee
	* Against targets in normal melee, on a botch, you hit a random wrong target.
	* If the targets are grappling or otherwise closely entangled, you always attack a random target.
* Cover
	* Normal Cover: ranged attacks take +D
	* Full Cover: ranged attacks take +D and only hit on a Crit.



## Special Cases
---
### Aquatic Combat

* Medium and larger weapons take +D to attack underwater.
* Bludgeoning weapons deal no damage.  Slashing weapons have +D to damage.  Piercing weapons deal normal damage.
* Missile and thrown weapons don't function underwater.  Fired into water, they have +D to attack against those Close to the surface, and can't penetrated deeper.
* Fire effects don't function.  Lightning effects become area of effect at the point they contact the water.
### Mounted Combat

* Combatants mounted on Large creatures are elevated in relation to Medium or smaller creatures on foot (no penalty to attack the mount).
