## Plot Threads & Relationships

* goals of notables
* opportunities
* developing crises / situations
* diplomatic relations
	* few of the other rulers want to deal with the Blind King, and there is little that others have that he wants.  When they do, he will deal with them to obtain it.
* ties to Dolmenwood


* The Blind King has no friends or allies, and permits no intruders in his realm.
	* He mostly just wants to listen and pursue his inscrutable project.  He is not interested in diplomacy.
	* Every so often, he conceives a desire for certain things to advance his work, usually rare reagents or magical treasures.  Sometimes secrets that have never been uttered, and must be wrung or tricked from someone, read aloud from a text, or described from observation.
* Mortals and faerie do come to Gloomhold to acquire things: secrets, prisoners, treasures.
	* Other Lords and Ladies will even send thieves to obtain what they want, though deny it.

* There are no rivals or uppity vassals in Gloomhold to intrigue with.  If his servants come seeking something the Blind King desires, they may be willing to engage in brief parley to get what they want.
	* In particular, being blind, they need other people to read or describe things they have been sent to reveal.
	* They usually start with threats, but will offer payment if it seems an easier path.

## Inhabitants

### The Blind King

* A terrible iron-crowned giant with grey, sagging skin, hair stiff with frost, and empty eye sockets.
* Though sightless, the Blind King senses the echoes of all that comes to pass within his drear realm, Gloomhold.
* All of the King’s servants are blind, for his countenance brings eternal death.
* The Blind King has no contact whatsoever with Dolmenwood and mercilessly imprisons any mortals who trespass in his realm.
* He has ruled here longer than any can remember, old and very powerful.


* All the Blind King's servants are blind: some eyeless, some with eyes sewn shut or plucked out, some blindfolded, or with metal plates or eyeless masks covering their faces.
	* They all navigate by an uncanny amplification of one or more other senses, usually hearing and/or smell, sometimes touch or taste.  Some have additional eldritch senses.
