Notables
* The Aubrathon, aka Merlinus Ambrosius



* The Drune proper are an ethnic group that has immigrated from Lyonesse.  They have magic woven into their culture, but are mostly just an insular people, kind of like gypsies.
* The Drune cabal is a wizardly mafia that is effectively the rulers / elders of the Drune people, but not all Drune people are part of it, knows its actions, or even like / approve of it.
	* Like Italian-Americans in the 1920s, some may admire the mafia, some aspire to join them for power and advancement, and others may turn to them when threats or troubles loom, as they can't expect much love from the Church, nobles, or general populace (many of whom believe all Drune people are evil sorcerers, devil-worshippers, etc).

* Need a name for the Drune cabal, to distinguish it from the ethnic group, even if most Dolmenwooders don't realize there is a distinction.

* To be able to destroy the Drune cabal, one would need to find replacement caretakers for the Ring of Chell.
	* If the Church could embrace theurgy, an order of wizard-priests could take over, though it would need to get up to speed quickly, and it has a long way to go from basically scratch.
	* The PCs could create a group to handle it.
	* The witches might be persuaded to take it over, but they would become more public and clash more with the Church, so that struggle would have to be settled in some fashion
	* A foreign sorcerer group might do it, but would they be any better neighbors?  The Church and witches would probably dislike them just as much.
	* A faerie lord might take it on, but they would need a worthy reason to do it.  What pact is struck, and with whom?  What would they demand from Dolmenwood and its people?
	* There might be some way to weave the Wood Gods into guardianship of the Ring.  This would empower them and impose more of their amoral, wild nature on the land and people, and perhaps indirectly empower the witches, some of whom might seek to borrow and exercise their power for their own ends.

* The right of Braithmaids to join the witches, over Drune objection, goes back to when the Drune originally arrived in Dolmenwood.
	* They were weak (newly driven out of Lyonesse in wizardly defeat), and had to make peace with the witches of the wood.
	* Part of this was agreeing their offspring could opt to join the witches (men and women both, although this is basically forgotten, as it tended to be women who opted out of the patriarchal Drune cabal's power).
	* Witches of some basic degree of competence (can't be complete hedge witches) may also marry into Drunish society, but must accept their law and culture, becoming Drunewives.  Few have ever done this, but it is allowed.
	* The Drune don't accept ordinary folk marrying in or their children marrying out (they would have to flee, and probably flee Dolmenwood entirely to get far enough away).
