
The Witches and Drune were both part of the Triple Compact (witches, drune, king/church)
* As a result, they are enjoined against messing with each other too much.  As strife happens, "presure" builds, and all fear what happens if it grows too strong, but not every butting of heads results in doom (thus the Aubrathon's fuckery was able to slide, though some diplomacy was needed; probably what got him exiled).
* Part of their deals permits every Drunish maiden (Braithmaid), before marriage, to leave her people and become a Witch, and she must be allowed to leave (though this doesn't always go entirely smoothly).


There are witches who are flirting with Atanuwe, the Flayed Queen, Cold Prince, and other dark powers
* they are some of the impetus the church's witch-hunting push, and also the witch-hunting has pushed some into the arms of such beings, for power, protection, revenge, etc.
* 
* "Harridans" are corrupted witches, now ogresses, serving Atanuwe
	* Shub's Nanna is one such, in service to Atanuwe
* 
* The witches are one of the few factions that knows the Cold Prince is stirring.  The Ambassador (and perhaps that human sage who longs for the Prince's return) have made some overtures to disgruntled witches.
	* The Prince knows they won't speak of him to either Drune or mortal society.
	* He has promised they will be free to tend to their Wood Gods under his renewed dominion, with Drune destroyed and mortals kneeling.
	* He promises there will a pact and faerie oaths to bind his word.
	* Some witches are enticed and may have actually signed on.  Others are tempted, but don't trust there isn't some faerie trickery to weasel out of the promise, or that they'll end up subjugated all the same through some back alley means.  Good-hearted witches can't countenance the suffering he'll inflict on mortals.
* 
* potential schism / civil war among the witches, as Celenia isn't pleased with them, and they seek to unseat her and/or corrupt the lay witches of the wood
* 
* some of these renegades are mad at the Drune or the Church/Nobles, but are constrained by current pacts from doing much about it.  Serving an unPacted patron offers a chance to bypass that.


Hedge Witches / Lay Sisters / Wise Women
* lots of minor folk in villages and towns, daughters in minor houses, etc; know a charm or two, some rituals, some herbalism / potion making, etc.
* they look to the more powerful witches for guidance, protection, and aid.  in return, they join rituals, trade herbs, and act as eyes and ears


The Hoaghryn
* a supposedly prophesied child who will become Queen of All Witches and lead a resurgence of Gwyrae and the power of the witches
* The current Queen of All Witches is pretty conflicted about this.
	* She's been publicly skeptical about the prophecy, but hasn't officially obstructed its seekers or adherents, fearing to look weak or divide the church (maybe pushing believers into the arms of other powers, or that they might go AWOL from her guidance, or do something dumb and disruptive and get the Church down on them).
	* She doesn't relish relinquishing power, but if there is such a child, should she?  But how would she know if it's real, and the child is really up to the task?
	* Atanuwe might seek to play on her fear and uncertainty to draw her into his service.
* Atanuwe knows of the prophecy from his harridans, and eagerly seeks the child to corrupt them and take over the witches through them.

The Queen of All Witches
* Celenia is successor to Nimue, who passed power to her and then departed from all knowledge
	* It is generally believed that Nimue sensed the end of her long life approaching and saw to the orderly transfer of power.
	* The current Queen doesn't believe that.  she doesn't know why Nimue left, or to where, or for what purpose.  It gnaws at her a bit.
	* Nimue was the most revered and powerful of witches; given witches' spotty record-keeping, she's the only other Queen any witch can even name.
