### St Abthius, Ever-Lustrous

* Miracle
	* St Abthius’ Rebuke (Ward Against Frost, Flame, and Storm)
	* As a young acolyte begging alms in a village, Abthius heard a commotion.  Finding a baker's apprentice fallen into his oven, he and climbed in to rescue him, emerging untouched by flame.  A a parish priest in the northlands, his town was repeatedly assailed by a trio drunken storm giants.  When he, rebuking their uncouth riot, stood unmoved by all their bellowing and crashing, they were chastised and returned to their mountains in peace.  As a bishop, Abthius came to aid King Thaldred II of Brackenwold. The Cold Prince had returned his frigid gaze to Dolmenwood, and Abthius—renowned for his fiery sermons—served as king's ambassador.  The fey mocked his sermons, and their liege waved his crystal wand, encasing the saint in ice. But Abthius's radiant beauty melted the fey frost, and he turned his back on the Prince, damning the fairy to the flames of perdition.

### St Quister, Ever-Loyal

* Miracle
	* St Quister's Horn (Command Water)
	* Quister sough peace after life as a soldier, and found it as a humble lighthouse keeper.  He passed his days in prayer and contemplation.  Despite the barren rock, his spring never failed to flow with clear water.  One stormy night, his flame was extinguished just as he spotted the lights of a fast-approaching ship.  Desperately blowing his horn and crying to God, he was answered as the tide surged up, carrying the ship safely over jagged reefs into the harbor, then sank down into placid calm.
	- Patronages: lamplighters, sailors, penitents.

### St Willofrith, the Wide

* Miracle
	* The Counsel of St Willofrith (Speak With Dead)
	* Willofrith was a tomb robber in his younger days, desecrating ancient barrows for riches. One day, he unearthed a sword of great beauty, engraved with ancient script and bedecked jewels. As reached for its hilt, a tomb's occupant spoke in a hollow voice, warning him of the sword's terrible curse.  Saved from doom, the young thief was converted to a man of God.
