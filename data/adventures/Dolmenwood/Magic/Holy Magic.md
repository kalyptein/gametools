## Theology

- The doctrine of the Church is not necessarily the cosmic truth of the Divine.
	- There is a benevolent force.
	- Some people can wield its power, some powerful wielders, saints, are bridges to it from beyond death.
		- Their bones, possession, etc are relics that hold some of their power.
	- Exact rules about behavior, gender, who can be a priest, etc may not be as the Church says.
- Clerics and Friars should roll their own theology.
	- They may bite their tongues in public to avoid being declared a heretic.  The Pluritines are a bit flexible about ideosyncratic theology, but not without orthodoxy.

Tags

Pious
* A person who espouses belief and has generally behaved in a faithful and virtuous manner (generosity to the Church and People, upright behavior, etc).  Holy casters are counted among the Pious.
	* A Pious individual may treat with other powers (arcane, faerie), as long as they aren't unholy, but must not partake in their veneration (holding faerie title or serving a lord, celebrating rites of Gwyrae, sacrificing to pagan gods, etc).

Unholy
* A being, object or spell that draws power from Hell or necromancy.

## Obtaining Miracles
---

- Each Saint is known for their signature miracle.  
	- A holy caster can attune to the saint and gain permanent access to it by:
		- Spending a night's vigil at their shrine (which must be in good, sanctified order).
		- Retrieving one of their relics and restore it to Church hands to serve the faithful.
		- Walking in the saint's footsteps, emulating their signature trial or suffering.
		- Facing serious hardship in worthy service to someone associated with the saint (keepers of their shrine, people or places they are a patron of, etc).
			- "Hardship" is relative to someone who could perform the miracle (e.g. Holy Fire is Tier 5, so it would require a deed worthy of a 9th level hero).
	- A holy caster can gain temporary access to a miracle while:
		- On holy ground during the saint's holy day.
		- Brandishing a relic of the saint while casting.

* A holy caster can obtain a miracle, even if they can't invoke it yet.

* The first time a Pious character stands vigil at a shrine, they gain access to the saint's miracle (up to Tier 3), and can invoke it like a priest.
	* Once it is Exhausted, access is gone.  To regain it, they must stand vigil at the shrine on the saint's holy day.

### (GM Note) Finding Shrines

* Active shrines are well known; most priests can guide you, and locals know their home shrine.
* Church lorekeepers and records can lead you to a rough area.
	* e.g. It was near the town of X, it was on the banks of the river Y in Aldweald, etc
	* These are vague or off by a few hexes.  The Nag-Lord's corruption has shifted things around, blurred records and memories, etc.
	* Records are not trivial to read; you have to find a batch of records and pour over them (decipher skill by thieves or mages might help) for clues about surroundings, proximity, famous people going there, etc.
	* Make it clear that shrines can come in many forms: housed in caves or grottos, in small buildings, chapels within larger structures, freestanding statues or icons.
* One might find the shell of a shrine where it originally was, without its icon (the Nag-Lord's confounding moved the icon, not the building).
	* Finding the icon and restoring it to that or new shrine is central to resanctifying it.  Usually they won't be too vastly far apart.

## Invoking Miracles
---

* A holy caster can invoke miracles of Tier <= (Level+1) / 2, rounding down.

* The invoker focuses their mind and verbally invokes the saint and call for their intervention.  There isn't a precise phrase, just a clear request.
	* The moment of concentration is deep enough that it Provokes.
* Roll CHA DC (10 + Tier).  Crits and botches have no effect beyond success and failure.
	* Success: the miracle takes place.
	* Failure: the invoker cannot attune, and the miracle does not occur.
		* If this miracle has already been invoked successfully since the invoker's last Long Rest, the invoker loses contact with this saint and their miracle is Exhausted.

* If the holy caster Exhausts 7 "high tier" miracles, their ability to invoke the saints is lost until they take a Long Rest (treat all miracles as Exhausted).
	* High Tier spells are those with Tier >= (max invokable Tier - 2)
		* e.g. For a Level 7 cleric, able to invoke up to Tier 4, Tier 2, 3, and 4 would be "high tier", Tier 1 would not.

Sacred Vow
* If a miracle is Exhausted, or an invocation fails, a holy caster may invoke / reroll by swearing to perform a service in the saint's name (fitting their idiom, aiding the field of their patronage, or generally doing or aiding good works).
	* Success: the invoker is bound to their vow, as by Holy Quest.
	* Failure: the invoker is disoriented, taking +D on rolls until they take a Brief Rest.

Refreshing Miracles
* After a night's sleep, you can pray in peace for an hour and refresh all Exhausted miracles (rolled into a Long Rest).

Divine Supremacy
* Holy magic can't be dispelled, suppressed, or countered by Arcane or Faerie magic of less than legendary power (Mighty Runes, Wishes, Faerie Lords, pagan godlings, Old Powers, Infernal nobility, archmages, etc).

## Holy Works
---
### Relics & Blessed Objects

* Major relics are magic items with powers of their own.
	* A Pious bearer can invoke the miracle of a major relic's saint (roll normally).  To refresh, they must stand vigil on holy ground and donate ??? to good works.
* Minor relics can be used to invoke the saint's miracle by a holy caster (basically a holy wand).
	* On a failed invocation (after at least one successful invocation through the relic), the relic is Exhausted until prayed over the following day.
	* On a botch, the relic is needed elsewhere and will soon vanished from the bearer's possessions when they aren't looking.
* Some relic items are just the bare relic (or more often, housed in a reliquary) and some have  the relic worked into an object (saint's teeth in a sword pommel, etc).

### Illuminated Prayers

* Dolmenwood has a tradition of short, ornately-illuminated texts dedicated to particular saints, with prayers, iconography, psalms, and holy passages on them.
* Anyone can hold the text and pray for the saint's miracle (roll to invoke).
	* Holy casters and Pious folk roll normally.  Those of questionable devotion roll with +D.
	* Succeed or fail, the text crumbles to dust.

Scribing Prayers
* As a downtime action which includes a Sabbath or holy day, a holy caster attuned to a saint can scribe an illuminated prayer to them (CHA DC 15; crit: make 1d3+1 copies).
	* This costs ??? for suitable inks, gold leaf, sacred oils and incense, etc.
	* Either the caster has (LVL >= Tier+2) or the downtime includes the saint's holy day.

### Blessed Panoply

Holy Incense
* When burned in swinging censor, it creates a Close cloud around you for 1 minute.
	* Unholy beings of Level 6 or less do their best not to enter the cloud.  Turning attempts against undead within the cloud have +A.
* 10 minutes of quiet prayer and meditation while burning holy incense prior to casting a miracle of up to Tier 2, gives +A to the casting roll.

Holy Water
* If an unholy being (undead, infernal) is splashed with holy water, they suffer 2d4 damage (usually irreducible).
* For 10 minutes, an unholy being of Level 6 or less cannot pass through a portal (window, door, etc) or pick up an object splashed with holy water.

Holy Oil
* To use holy oil, the subject is anointed and prayed over by one of the Pious as an action.
* An anointed character has resistance the next time they resist unholy power (undead, infernal, necromancy) within the following hour.
	* If already afflicted by unholy power, holy oil grants retroactive resistance (reroll, etc).
	* Holy oil can only offer benefit once per affliction.
* An anointed weapon is able to harm unholy beings for 10 minutes as if it were silver or magic.

Creating Blessed Panoply
* As a downtime action including the Sabbath, on holy ground, a ritual use of the Bless miracle can craft blessed items (60% of full cost).
* With costly materials, a holy forge, suitable skill, and time, you can craft blessed weapons, arrows, shields, armor, etc.
