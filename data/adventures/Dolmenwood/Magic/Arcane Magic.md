
## Cosmology
---

* Arcane power issues from the Earthly world, below Heaven: the lower air, the land and seas, the cthonic and infernal.
	* Heaven and Faerie stand apart the sphere of the Earth and beyond the orbit of the Moon.
* Practitioners call upon hidden sympathies and secret properties, shape the flow of invisible energies, and bind them up with thought and word.

Gwyrae & Witches
* An ancient religion of Europe, of magicians who studied the Old Powers, or "Gwyrigons", embodied elemental beings of water, wold, hill, and valley, less human than most pagan gods of antiquity.
	* Gwyrigons have a locus, a place where the godling is immanent.
* These practitioners gained power through their study and interceded between Gwyrigons and humanity as they saw fit.  Many were guides, seers, and counselors to their communities, some wielded power for their own ends.
* As Gwyrae was displaced by other religions, and eventually Christianity, its practitioners, now known as witch, have faded into the background.  Mostly passed along among women, out of sight of the Church, witchcraft is sought on the sly by those who need medicine for a loved one, guidance for their future, or a curse for an enemy.

* A witch's initiation occurs over 13 lunar cycles; the final ending with a night-long psychedelic visionary ritual and induction into a coven.
* Some witches pledge fealty to one Gwyrigon, taking it as an Eldritch Patron and wielding its powers more directly.  Such a witch is an "eye of their Gwyrigon", a channel for it to perceive through.
* Dreams are the channel through which one communes with the Gwyrigons.  It is taboo among witches to speak of ones dreams, or ask other witches about them.
* The practice of witchcraft tends toward the earthy and commonplace, meant to blend in among ordinary life: cauldrons, baking, carved talismans, spells woven at the loom, straw poppets, and rune-etched kitchen knives.  Chants are sung in common language or Witches' Cant, descended from ancient Celtic and Breton language.

Hermeticism & Wizards
* Hermetic magic is an amalgamation of lore from across the known world: Egyptian Hekau, alchemy, Solomonic seals, astrology, Qabalah, Norse Runes, Christian demonology and angelology, Zoroastrian amulets, and anything else practitioners can glean.
* Their workings tend toward the formal and intricate: elaborate magic circles, rune-carved amulets and staves, invocations of Names of Power, complex passages incanted in Latin, Greek, Arabic, Hebrew, or Egyptian, etc.
* The Church looks askance at magic, so most modern wizards pose as simple astrologers, scholars, and physicians.  Observing the divinely-ordained world for its patterns, and using its herbs for healing is permitted.
* Wizards organize into cabals of fellow-seekers, cults in serve to an Eldritch Patron, or isolated masters and apprentices.

### Sense Magic

* INT | WIS DC 11, Casting (1 minute), Focus (round), Refresh (short), Self
* You can sense the presence of Closeby magic spells, items, unconcealed magical beings, and magically-infused features.
	* Continued scrutiny on a specific presence may yield deeper insight.

## Casting Arcane Spells
---

* An arcane caster can cast spells of Tier <= (Level+1) / 2, rounding down.

* The caster focuses their mind while chanting and making arcane gestures (must have at least one hand free or holding an implement such as a wand).
	* This is complex enough that it Provokes.
* Roll INT DC (10 + Tier).
	* Crit: the spell is cast and you may double one of the spell’s numerical effects (damage dice, range, area, duration) or gain +A to its Focus rolls.
		* Some spells have particular benefit options or mandatory choices in their description.
	* Success: the spell is cast.
	* Failure: you falter and fail to weave the spell.
		* If this spell has already been cast successfully since the caster's last Long Rest, the arcane structure of the spell unravels and it is Exhausted.
	* Botch: as Failure and an arcane mishap occurs.
		* Some spells have particular specific mishaps in their description.

* If the caster Exhausts 7 "high tier" spells, their ability to channel magic is lost until they take a Long Rest (treat all spells as Exhausted).
	* High Tier spells are those with Tier >= (max invokable Tier - 2)
		* e.g. For a Level 7 magician, able to cast up to Tier 4, Tier 2, 3, and 4 would be "high tier", Tier 1 would not.

Consume Reagent
* You can weave a reagent into a spell (equal or higher Tier than the spell, with an aligned aspect), gaining +A on the casting.  The reagent turns to ash or is otherwise consumed.

Wild Magic
* If a spell is Exhausted, a casting fails, or the caster craves greater power, they may throw caution to the wind and Call Forth Wild Magic (once per casting).
	* The spell becomes Dangerous until you take a Long Rest and refresh your spells.  If it was already Dangerous, it is now also Hindered.
	* Exhausted Spell: cast the spell anyway.  The spell is still Exhausted.
	* Failed Cast: reroll the casting and use the new result.  You can't do this with naturally Dangerous spells.
	* Greater Power: roll to cast normally.  If successful, treat it as a Crit.

Refreshing Spells
* After a night's sleep, you can meditate and perform arcane rituals for an hour to refresh all Exhausted spells (rolled into a Long Rest).

## Arcane Research
---
Insights
* Insight are aspected, intangible bits of arcane lore used to learn spells.
	* Aspects: Fire, Water, Protection, Illusion, War, Waxing, Binding, Hell, Journeys, etc.
		* A "general insight" cost can be paid with any kind of insight.
	 * Power: a special kind of insight needed to learn higher Tier spells.
* Gaining Insights
	* You gain insights by studying magical phenomena, fonts of power, magical beings, magic items, and similar things.
		* A given instance can be studied once, but the same type can be studied repeatedly.
		* Consumable items (potions, rare fungi, reagents) are used up in studying them.
		* You can study new sources of spells you already know to gain insight.
		* Some subjects require a check to successfully study, some don't.
	* After a casting Crit or mishap from a Tier 3+ spell, roll (INT+spell's Tier) DC 18 to gain a Power insight.
		* Crits from using Wild Magic don't grant insight.
		* A mishap must leave you capable of contemplating/studying it to make the roll.

Learning a Spell
* Roll INT against a DC based on how you are learning (source or tutor; experimentation doesn't involve a roll).
	* Success: you learn the spell.
	* Failure: you learn the spell but it is Dangerous to cast.
		* The next time you try to study the spell (by any means), take -3 DC.
		* If you successfully cast the spell under stressful conditions (i.e. adventuring), your next downtime action of study is automatically successful.
* Spell Cost (for tutelage and arcane experimentation; sources don't cost insight)
	* Tier 1-2 (2 general insights)
	* Tier 3-4 (4 general insights, 1 power insight)
	* Tier 5 (6 general insights, 2 power insights)

Spell Sources
* A source is an arcane object which conveys the esoteric insight a magician needs to gain access to one or more particular spells.
	* Tomes containing arcane research notes and esoteric lore.
	* Sites of power, such as rune-carved standing stones, petroglyphs, etc.
* Learning a spell from a source is a DC 15 downtime action.
	* To learn from a tome, you must have ready access to it.
	* To learn from a site, you must personally have spent a few hours studying it, taking notes, sketches, and samples (1 slot) to work with later.
	* Learning from a source does not cost insight.

Tutelage
* Arcane tutors, such as magicians and demons, can teach spells they know.
* Learning from a tutor is a DC 18 downtime action (for both you and the tutor).
	* Adding the mentor's INT and CHA to the roll.
	* When you first begin learning the spell, you must pay the the spell's insight cost (the tutor may also contribute insights).

Arcane Experimentation
* You can perform arcane experimentation as a downtime action.
	* You may experience boons or banes, similar to a Carousing action.
	* You gain a chance to learn a spell from a random pool of possibilities by paying the insight cost.
	* If you pay the usual monetary cost, you can also gain XP as if carousing.
* Roll 1d8 + modifiers
	* Do not apply Renown or modifiers for most normal holidays and carousing facilities.
	* Do add modifiers for money paid, fonts of power, conjunctions, research partners, quality libraries and labs, unseasons, etc.
		* You may spend 1 general insight to gain +1 to the roll.
		* You may spend 1 reagent to gain +((Tier+1)/2, rounding up) to the roll.
	* You gain benefits and mishaps as per the Carousing table, but the particulars come from the Arcane Experimentations tables.
		* Benefits may include gaining insights, extra spells to choose from, crafting consumables as a byproduct, oracular knowledge, etc.
		* Mishaps can include rogue spells, accidental summonings, spoiled reagents, etc.
* Roll a pool of 3 random, unknown spells.  You may choose 1 and pay its insight cost to learn it.
	* The first spell of the 3 rolled is always available to choose.
	* To choose either of the other spells, pay a suitably aspected insight.
		* When working with a font, conjunction, library, ley line, faerie realm, lunar phase, unseason, or other resource with aligned aspect to a spell, this can cover the cost.
* Determining a Random Spell
	* Roll d6s for the spells' Tiers.  For each, the researcher may accept it, or reroll and take the new result.  A roll of 6 allows the researcher to choose the Tier.
	* Roll a random spell from the Tier (reroll any known spells).

## Arcane Crafting
---
### Reagents

* Reagents have a Tier and usually take up 1 slot (including secure packing, preservation, etc).
	* Reagents can be broken into parts, dividing their Tier (a Tier 3 could become 3x Tier 1 or a Tier 1 and Tier 2), but can't usually be combined.
* Extracting
	* Reagents can be obtained by dissecting corpse of magical beings and collecting samples from fonts of power or magical phenomena.
		* Dissection
			* A corpse yields a single reagent (Tier = ((Level / 2) - 1, rounding up).  Some corpses might offer a choice of multiple possible reagents (eyes, heart, etc) with different aspects and uses.
			* Roll (INT+DEX) DC 18 to extract the reagent.  Reaching DC 13 gives a damaged reagent with half the full Tier (50% rounding up, 50% rounding down).
				* The dissection can be performed by an ally (their DEX) under the magician's guidance (your INT).
		* Samples
			* Fonts of power (magical terrain features) and phenomena have either a limited number of reagents that can be gathered (perhaps regenerating slowly over time) or the gathering can only be performed under special conditions (a full moon, when freshly watered with the blood of a Level 6+ being, etc).
			* Depending on the circumstances, a check may be required to collect the reagent.  The Tier will depend on the power of the source.

### Brewing Potions

* "Potion" includes oils, salves, incenses, pigments, cooked food, etc.
	* Potion effects are "passive", you use the potion and an effect occurs.  It isn't guided by will or intention.

* You need a pot, fire, a reagent, and minor ingredients.
	* Describe the property you're trying to evoke from your reagent; it should align with an aspect of the reagent.
	* Minor ingredients include water, oil, herbs, charcoal, salt, lime, etc.  A brewer will usually have them on hand, or they can be gathered from the environment.
		* Special minor ingredients may alter the brewing process or product.
* As part of a Short Rest, you can brew a potion.  Roll INT DC (10 + reagent's Tier).
	* Crit: choose 1
		* High yield: make 1d3+1 doses of potion
		* Develop a Recipe: +A when you make this potion with these ingredients and reagent.
		* Discovery: gain an insight with an aspect from the reagent.  If it was T3-4, also gain a power insight, if T5, gain 2 power insights.
	* Success: make 1 dose of the potion
	* Failure: make 1 dose of the potion, but it has a side-effect.
		* Side-effect possibilities: minor casting mishaps, magic item flaws/curses.
	* Botch: make 1 dose of the potion, but it has serious side-effect(s).
* Options
	* Take +A if you spend an aligned insight or mix in an extra reagent with aligned aspect.
	* Take +2 to brewing if the potion's effect directly evokes the reagent's source.
		* e.g. brewing a Potion of Fire Breath from dragon's blood.
	* Brewing in a quality lab can grants bonuses.
	* Take +D if the effect is a stretch for the reagent and its aspects, but the GM allows it.
	* Extra Reagents: some rare and powerful potions may require multiple reagents.

* Anyone can use a potion, simply by consuming / applying it.

### Scribing Scrolls

* "Scroll" can include tokens and other kinds of consumable items.  You draw sigils on an object with magic ink to enchant it, and it is activated by incantation.
	* Scroll effects are like spells, guided by the invoker's will and intentions.

* Brew magical ink as a potion.
	* Give it a name based on the reagent, e.g. harpy-blood ink.  It has a Tier equal to the reagent used in brewing.  Infer some aspects for the ink; there can be more than one.
	* If brewing resulted a potion with side-effects, scrolls made with it have side-effects (perhaps an option from the spell mishap table for the Tier below the spell's Tier, or equal Tier, if the ink was botched).
* As part of a Short Rest, you can use the ink to scribe a scroll with a spell.
	* You must know the spell, and an aspect of the ink must align with it.
	* Reduce the ink's Tier by the Tier of the spell.  You can't mix inks to combine their Tiers, but you can use one batch of ink to make multiple, lower Tier scrolls.

* Anyone can invoke a scroll / token by making an INT casting check for it's spell.
	* Failure: the spell is not activated, but the scroll / token isn't consumed.
	* Botch: the scroll / token is consumed and a mishap occurs.

### Crafting Wands

* A magician can only have 3 wands (or similar spell-casting talismans) that they have crafted.  To make a new one, one of the old must be broken.
	* One can have any number of wands obtained from elsewhere, the limit is only on the number a magician can have made.  Wands enter the world because when a magician dies, their wands remain.

* Crafting a wand for a spell you know of (up to Tier 3) requires a suitably aspected reagent of the spell's Tier, (Tier x 100) gp, and a downtime action with access to tools.
	* Roll to cast the spell with +A.
		* Crit: the wand has an additional boon.
		* Success: the wand is crafted.
		* Failure: the wand has a flaw.
		* Botch: the wand has 2 flaws.
* Crafting wands for spells above Tier 3 requires rare and special means.

## Eldritch Patrons *

* Eldritch Patrons are powerful magical beings a magician can form pacts with, providing offerings or services in return for arcane knowledge and boons.
	* Demons, djinn, elementals, pagan gods, Old Powers, mighty undead, ascended magicians, faerie lords, etc.
	* Some patrons accept clients who are non-magicians.
		* If a patron accepts a Knight, the Knight must take them as their liege.
		* Clerics, Friars and others of Pious nature can never take an Eldritch Patron without abandoning their faith.

* Patrons vary in the process of forming the pact, and who the patron wishes to deal with.
	* There is usually a prolonged ritual, including offerings, trials, or services performed for the patron.  The client ends up with a mark of some kind, indicating their bond.

Benefits
* Each patron can act as a source for a collection of its iconic spells.  The client can commune with it through drugs, meditation, and oracles to learn its spells during downtime.

### Tier 1

Alarm
* T1, 1 day, A Close skull
* You enchant a skull to shriek when it perceives something you designate.  It has the perceptions and judgement of the skull's original owner (human, animal, etc).
	* e.g. leave it watching a door, with instructions to shriek if anyone opens it without saying a password.  Place it in a passage to shriek if it hears any noise louder than a whisper. Etc.

Breath
* T1, 1 hour, Self
* You can hold your breath for the duration.  You must still breathe to speak and cast spells.

Burning Hands
* T1, Instant, Close
* You unleash an arc of flame, between a narrow jet and a full circle around yourself, dealing 1d6 fire and igniting flammables.

Charm
* T1, 1d8 days, A Nearby creature with a mind
* The subject is enchanted and regards you as a close friend, and forgets you just cast a spell on them.  This doesn't remove any other allegiances or attitudes.
	* P [3] vs R [Level+WIS]
	* The spell ends if the subject notices you or your allies harming or threatening it (physically, socially, emotionally, financially).
	* At the spell's end, if you haven't done anything to cause them to behave in ways they now regret, they don't realize you enchanted them.  If you did, they now remember that you cast a spell on them.
* Ritual (1 minute)
	* You enchant a small object, which remains empowered for 1 hour.  If the target accepts this as a non-trivial gift, without payment or gift in return, they are affected by Charm with P [LVL+CHA].

Colour Spray
* T1, Instant, Near
* A flare of many-coloured light burst out in an arc, between a narrow jet and a full circle around yourself.  Onlookers in range are blinded for 1 round.  Make a dispelling contest against any invisibility magics within range.

Corpse Servant
* T1, 1 hour, A Close corpse
* The corpse (which must have at least 3 intact limbs and a head) rises as a zombie or skeleton and obeys your commands, acting on your turn.  You can only have one such servant at a time.
	* When the spell ends, the corpse collapses into grave dust.

Crystal Resonance
* T1, A Close crystal (50 gp or more)
* Choose 1:
	* Imprint: the crystal is imprinted with some or all of the energies of its surroundings (sound, temperature, light).  It must remain in place for 10 minutes.
	* Release: the crystal emits its imprinted energies into the environment Nearby, replaying up to 10 minutes of sound, emitting light, heating or cooling, etc.  The effect lasts as long as the crystal remains in position.
		* This cannot produce energies strong enough to cause harm, blindness, etc.  It may be overwhelmed by current ambient conditions if they are extreme enough.
		* An imprint can be used more than once, but each time the spell ends, there is a 5% chance the crystal shatters.

Detect Magic
- T1, Sustain (10 minutes), Nearby magics
- Enchanted objects, areas, or creatures are wreathed in shimmering colours for all to see.
	- The colours and flux reveal various details of the magic to a knowledgeable onlooker.  It takes a round to get simple details, minutes to discern more complex facets.
	- Enchantments of deception or concealment are not illuminated.
	- While the spell lasts, you can evoke or suppress the effect, or limit it to a smaller area.

Elixir
* T1, 1 hour, A Close vial
* Enchants a simple brew of common herbs (easily gathered in any wood or market).  Consumed or splashed on an injury, it heals 2d4+LVL Guard.
	* If the recipient immediately takes a Brief Rest, they heal an additional 2d4+LVL Guard and remove a condition that would naturally end within a few hours.

Eyebite
* T1, Instant, A Nearby sighted creature
* The target take 1d4 spirit damage and can't see you until the end of its next turn.

Fairy Servant
* T1, 1 hour, Near
* You summon an invisible fairy sprite.  If given a small gift (a mug of booze, a ration, colorful flattery, an interesting pebble, a carved stick, etc) it performs tasks as bidden.
	* It can cook, clean, fetch, transcribe, etc.  It can lift and fly (Near speed) with an item of up to 1 slot.  It can be given standing orders and left to pursue them, or sent on tasks.
	* It is mute.  It won't inflict harm, and is banished if harmed.
	* If gifted a a finer offering (a pretty trinket of 50 gp, a bit of fine food or drink, or a portion of magical herb or fungus), it will magically repair a mundane object, given the pieces.

Fog
* T1, Focus (1 minute), Close
* The area Close to you fills with fog (light obscuration).
	* You have +A to stealth in foggy or shadowy conditions.  Ranged attacks have +D against target within or behind the cloud.
	* While you will, the cloud moves with you, but you can leave it in place and move on.

Glyph of Sealing
* T1, Sustain (1 hour), Near

	* If the glyph is carefully crafted into the object, spell is Permanent (dispelling only suppresses it for 1 hour).  You may also enchant tokens that allow a bearer to open and close it as you can.

Gingerbread Charm
* T1, 1 day, A Close baked figurine
* A bread figurinee (up to 1') baked within the last day animates and obeys your instructions with human intelligence.  It is curious and mischievous, within the bounds of its orders.  While Close, it can communicate with you telepathically.
	* Gingerbread is traditional, but any baked good will do.

Hypnotize

Magic Missile
* T1, Instant, Far
* A glowing bolt of force hurtles to your target, dealing 1d4 damage.

Oak, Ash, Thorn
* T1, Focus (10 minutes), Self
* Faerie, arcane beings (elementals, devils, summonings), and undead can't attack, and their magics cannot affect you.  If you attack or use magic on such a being, the spell ends.
* You are immune to possession, beguiling, and compulsion magic, and cannot be detected by arcane or faerie magic, or by occult senses (telepathy, smelling mortality, etc).

Oxidize
* T1, Instant, Close

Puppet
* T1, Focus (round), A Nearby creature
* The target becomes ensnared and mimics all your movements, acting on your turn instead of its normal one.

	* If mimicking you would directly harm it or an ally, it may resist with CHA DC 15.  Success allows it to resist, but doesn't end the spell.

Shadowplay
* T1, Focus (10 minutes), Near
* You conjure a convincing visible and audible illusion of up to Medium size.  It can move and act under your mental command (and may move up to Near from where it first appeared), and passive reacts to the environment in plausible ways (reflects firelight, ripples int he wind, casts shadows, etc)
	* If a viewer has reason to mistrust the illusion (weird behavior, outlandishness), they may resist with WIS DC (your last casting/focus result) to realize it is illusory.
	* A brief brush against the illusion won't immediately reveal it, but might be grounds to mistrust it.  More significant contact automatically reveals its intangibility.
	* If a viewer knows the illusion is false, it takes on a translucent appearance and can be seen through.

Shield
* T1, 1 hour, Self
* A field of arcane force surrounds you, granting you defense 14 (18 on a Crit) and immunity to Tier 1 spells (and the powers of creatures of Level 2 or lower) originating from beyond Close range.
* Counter (Instant)
	* Shield can be cast as a counter to any attack or effect with a physical component, a momentary flash of shield to deflect harm.  Against attacks, use the casting roll as defense if it is better than your normal defense. Against spells, roll a normal counter contest.

Translate
* T1, 1 hour, A Nearby person

	* This spell can't translate magical or supernatural languages.

Vapours of Dream
