## Glamours & Runes
---
### Obtaining Glamours

* Glamours are innate magical talents possessed by those with faerie blood.  They are not learned, but emerge spontaneously as one's power develops.
* When rolling to gain a random Glamour, roll 2 options and the player chooses one.

### Obtaining Runes

* Each Faerie Realm has a unique Rune, with ascending Tiers of usage.
* Via deeds, one claims "crests" associated with the Realm, unlocking access to its Tiers of power (each crest grants +1 Tier).
	* Lesser Rune powers are Tier 1.
	* Greater Rune powers are Tier 3.
	* Might Rune powers are Tier 5.

#### Realm Crests

* Knave's Crest
	* Physical tokens possessed by the common faeries bound to the Realm.
	* They can only be given voluntarily; coercion (or trickery, extortion, wagers, etc) can be used, but not magical compulsion.  Even tokens without powers will be valuable to the faerie.  They can't be replaced trivially, and won't be given lightly.
* Knight's Crest
	* Physical tokens possessed by the faerie Gentry of the Realm.
	* They obey the same strictures as Knave's Crests, but are more likely to possess at least a little overt magical power as well.
* King's Crest
	* A faerie with deep ties and power within the realm (usually its ruler, but sometimes others) can bestow up to 3 King's Crests (never without some return benefit).
	* King's Crests take the form of titles or epithets associated with the Realm.
		* A title (and crest) can be abdicated by the bearer's free will..  Lords cannot revoke titles, and are forbidden from slaying those who bear titles of their Realm, but may curse or torment them (and/or their friends and family) until they "willingly" abdicate.
* Gate Crest
	* Touching the gates of, or setting foot within, the Realm grants this irrevocable crest.
* Road Crest
	* Journeying halfway into the depths of the Realm grants this irrevocable crest.
* Heart Crest
	* Journeying to the heart of the Realm grants this irrevocable crest.

Notes
* Knave's and Knight's Crests are physical items and can be lost.  Multiple Knave Crests and/or multple Knight's Crests only count as one of their type, but offer redundancy.
* All permanent magic items tied to a Faerie Realm, preexisting or self-crafted, count as Crests of a rank commensurate with their power.
* To "possess" a Knave's or Knight's Crest, it can be physically on you, or safely ensconced in your sanctum (home, etc) or a trove untouched by any other.
* Faerie are bound to their rank, and cannot hold or bestow a Crest of another rank, nor compel a subordinate to give one.
* If you have more than 5 Realm Crests for a Realm, additional gifts from it will find their way to you: retainers, demesnes, items, boons, etc.

#### Free Crests

* Each level an Enchanter grants 1 Free Crest.
	* As long as you have at least basic knowledge of a Realm, you can invest up to 2 Free Crests in it.
	* Once invested in a Realm, you can't reassign a Free Crest except: 
		* When you gain a Realm Crest, you can release a Free Crest invested in that Realm.
		* When you gain a level, you can release 1 crest (at 2nd-4th), 2 crests (at 5th-9th), or 3 crests (at 10th or each paragon rank).

### Invoking Glamours

* Glamours are activated through act of will (taking an action), without gesture or speech, except as noted.  This does NOT Provoke.
	* Glamours don't usually require / allow a casting roll, and you can't Call Forth Wild Magic.
* Except as noted, Glamours can be used at-will.

### Invoking Runes

* Runes are activated through an act of will (taking an action), without gesture or speech, except as noted.  This does Provoke.
* Runes always manifest and don't require a casting check, but sometimes involve CHA rolls for efficacy.

Usage by Level
* 1-4) Lesser (once a day), Greater (once a season), Mighty (once ever)
* 5-9)  Lesser (twice a day), Greater (once a week), Mighty (once a decade and a year)
* 10+) Lesser (thrice a day), Greater (once a day), Mighty (once a year and a day)

## The List of Glamours

### Awe

- Glamour, 1d4 rounds, Limited (10 minutes), Nearby mortals and beasts
- Your dramatic gesture and otherworldly mien strike fear or awe (your choice) in a group with a sum of Levels up to your own Level (lowest levels first).
	- Targets roll CHA DC 15 or are overcome with the emotion.
	- If you speak a single person's name as you invoke, they have +D to the check.

### Cloak of Darkness

- Glamour, Focus (action) + 1 round, Limited (10 minutes)
- You conjure a cloak of darkness (of voluminous but relatively normal size) and can drape it over yourself or another person or object (even covering a window, door, etc).  Attention slides away from it, leaving the covered subject invisible.
	- If the subject moves or takes action, or a bright light (torch, etc) is brough Closeby, your Focus is broken.

### Coin Toss

- Glamour, Limited (10 minutes), A tossed gold or silver coin
- You propose an immediate course of action, then flip a coin and left it fall to the ground.
	- Heads and tails indicate a likelihood of weal or woe within 10 minutes.  Rolling / wobbling before settling, indicates some ambiguity.  Landing on edge indicate true balance.  If neither is present, the coin rolls out of sight.  If the question is somehow invalid or hidden, the coin vanishes with a pop at the height of the toss.
- Reusing this in the same general area or about the same topic, within a day, has a 50% chance of giving a random outcome.

### Crooked Tongue

- Glamour, Limit (1 hour), until sleep or dismissed (fades after 10 minutes), Self
- You can speak and understand a non-magical language you hear.  You can only speak and write falsehoods (in any language) while your maintain the enchantment.

### Defy the Depths

- Glamour, Limited (10 minutes), Focus (action), A Nearby creature or object
- The subject becomes buoyant in water.
	- Subjects up to Large fully float.  Larger objects can be lightened, but won't fully float.
	- On yourself, allows you to walk slowly and carefully atop water, mud, quicksand, snow, etc.

### Disguise Object

- Glamour, Limit (up to Level targets at a time), until broken or dismissed, A Close object
- You place a convincing illusion over the object, making it seem like something different of roughly similar dimensions.
	- If anyone else touches it (more than a brief brush), the illusion fades over 1 round.

### Door into Shadow

- Glamour, A Close patch of shadow or darkness
- Search a Close-sized patch of shadow, rolling WIS DC 15, to find a shadow door.
	- Once found, shadow doors remain where they are for an hour.  Each can only be used once, to enter or exit, before fading away, leaving that patch empty.
	- Entering, you (and a familiar, but no others) can roam the halls of shadow.  Roll WIS each round to find a known shadow door within Far of your entry point (DC 15) or a random door within twice Far (DC 12).
		- On the fifth round in the halls, the things that live there catch your scent.

### Fairy Dust

- Glamour, Limited (24 hours), 1 day
- You conjure a small quantity of glittering dust into a pot, pouch, cupped hand, etc.
	- It will fill a Close radius if flung, or can be sprinkled over a Near area.
	- The dust is clingy and faintly luminous, but normally only visible to faeries and enchanters. Sprinkling a pinch in someone elses lets them see it until they next sleep.
- Some possible uses: leaving invisible signs.  Revealing invisible things (lasts 1 minute before the concealing magic overcomes it).  Clings to hands or feet, leaving tracks.  Dusting a surface grants +A to find secret doors or subtle features.

### Flame Charm

- Glamour
- You can conjure and extinguish small flames.
	- (Focus (action), Self) A colorful candle-flame burns on your fingertip.
	- (Instant, A Close flame (up to torch-sized)) You snaps your fingers and extinguish a flame.
		- You may have it flare before going out.  Close onlookers are blinded for a round.

### Fool's Gold

- Glamour, Limit (up to Level x 20 "coins" per day), 1d6 minutes, Close object(s)
- You transform  small, vaguely coin-sized bits of material (pebbles, wood chips, copper coins) into coin types of your choosing, or an object of similar weight becomes precious metal.

### Forgetting

- Glamour, Limit (1/day/target), A Nearby mortal creature of up to Level (LVL+CHA).
- With a twinkle of your eye, the target forgets the events of the prior round.

### Masquerade

- Glamour, Sustain, Self
- You alter the appearance of your head (face, hair, eyes, etc) and voice with illusion.
	- Minor changes take 1 round, major ones take up to 10 minutes.
	- You cannot duplicate someone's appearance or voice exactly; it's never quite right.

### Mirror Meld

* Glamour, Limit (one enchantment at a time), A Close reflective surface
* You enchant a reflective surface with an effect.
	* Pluck out a mundane object's reflection.  It is real for Sustain (10 minutes).
	* Tuck a real object you can lift in one hand into the reflection, where it is always visible.  Call it to hand when your image is also in the reflection.  Disrupting the reflection expels the object.
	* Step into a reflection and emerge through another one Nearby.
	* Leave your image in the reflection, perhaps out of the way, but always somewhat visible.  While within Far, you can sense when someone Closeby is reflected or speaks.  You can Focus (total) to perceive through your image and make it move and speak.

### Mirth and Malice

- Glamour, Limit (1/day/person), length of interaction + 10 minutes, Nearby mortals
- You subliminally infuse your social interaction with a group with either good cheer, creeping discord and anger, or anxiety and unease, either subtle or strong.
	- After interacting at least a minute or so, make a CHA+LVL check.  Target that fail a WIS+LVL resistance feel the emotion .  Your behavior needn't obvious convey the emotion, it just creeps in beneath.
	- You don't control how they act, but might manage to steer their interpretation based on your interaction.  A subtle emotion remains subliminal, a strong one is acute, though not overwhelming.

### Moon Sight

- Glamour, Permanent, Self
- The area Nearby is illuminated by full moonlight only you can see.

### Savor

- Glamour, Limit (1/day/subject), Permanent, A visible or named mortal or animal
- Conjure a palmful of faerie spice.  Sprinkled across food or drink, it smells and tastes delicious to the subject, and you may impart a charm.
	- After consuming it, their disposition toward you improves one step.
	- They become somewhat drowsy after a moment; becoming distracted or nodding off if there's no reason not to.
	- The treat tastes delicious for a moment, then utterly vile.
- Instead of sprinkling it, you can make it can coalesce into their favorite treat (sweets, pastries, sizzling sausage, etc), with the same magic in it.
	- Conjured treats are real and nourishing, but small.  Eating nothing else, a person will  linger on, weak with hunger, but never actually starving to death.

### Seeming

* Glamour, until broken or dismissed, Self
* By illusion, you can adjust the appearance of your clothing.
	* Doesn't change properties (weight, warmth, etc) but the clothes behave realistically (actually sodden clothes don't seem to drip, but leaves puddles; cloaks flow in the breeze, filthy garments don't smell, etc).
	* A brief brush doesn't dispel the illusion, but a more solid touch does.

### Silver Tongue

- Glamour, 1d4 rounds, Limited (1/day/subject), A Nearby mortal who can understand your words
- You speak briefly and the listener (of Level up to your Level+CHA) regards your words as trustworthy.
	- You don't change their perceptions or memory, and you can't dictate how they react, given their belief in what you say.

### Subtle Sight

- Glamour, Sustain (round), Safe, Refresh (round), Self
- Concentrate and roll WIS | CHA DC 11 to see invisible and ethereal things, and to pierce Glamour and Tier 1 illusions or transformations.

### Through the Keyhole

- Glamour, Limit (1/day per door/wall), Self
- Peering closely at a keyhole, peephole, crack beneath a door, chink in a wall, narrow window, etc, you teleport through to the other side.
	- Don't need to actually see through, but the hole must go all the way through the surface, with room for you to appear (you can feel if it'll work).

### Vanishing

- Glamour, Limit (1/day/target), 1d3 rounds, A creature within Far
- You disappear from the target's perceptions.  Your light sources still illuminate the area.
	- If you attack or cast a spell on the target, you reappear.

### Whistling Wind

- Glamour, Limit (10 minutes), Focus (minute), The Nearby air
- You can command a brisk breeze to blow, enough to weakly manipulate things (blow out candles, riffle pages, carry a sheet of parchment, etc).
	- It can carry sounds from one place to another: whispers from a speaker to a listener, sounds away from unwanted listeners, misdirect as to the origin of a sound, etc.

### Word Given

- Glamour, Until ended, A Close pair of people
- Parties (possibly including you) make a willing agreement and you invoke this glamour as you witness it.
	- If any party knowingly breaks the agreement, you and all participants know who, but not how.  If they broke it and only realize later, the awareness occurs then.
	- If the deal is completed / obviated / waived, you and all participants know it, and the glamour ends.

Summon Goblin Merchant

* Greater, until business is concluded, A close "passageway"
* You issue a request into a hole, well, pot, doorway, hollow stump, etc, and shortly a goblin merchant pops out, bearing an enormous, overstuffed sack.
	* They linger 1d6 hours (longer if particularly enticed), departing through any available hole once dealings are done.  They flee at any sign of danger.
	* They are warded against beguiling, charm, and deception, and can (mostly) spot lies or frauds.  They are very elusive and tricksy if threatened.
	* If mistreated, the Goblin Market denies future summons, and goblin thieves come to rob and ruin the offender until the harm is repaid sevenfold.  Restitution is very expensive, but enough riches will be persuasive.
	* They don't offer charity and always drive a hard bargain, but for a good customer, may offer credit or accept a promise of future payment (witnessed with Word Given).
* Merchant services include...
	* Selling
		* Mundane goods (food, drink, adventuring gear, household goods, arms and armor, pipeweed, etc) at normal price.
		* Consumable magic items and reagents (a handful of selections on offer).
		* Information (maps, secrets, advice, lore).
	* Buying (or pawning)
		* Magic items, consumables, reagents.
		* Information that could lead to profit or security.
		* Treasure (gems, art, jewelry)
	* Services
		* Money changing (coins and gems, 5% service fee, or free with other dealings)
		* Repairs and customizations with their portable forge and tools.
		* Storage of either coin (free) or items (upfront fee of the greater of 10% value or 10 gp, extra if troublesome) for up to a year and day.

Royal Audience

* Mighty, until business is concluded, Self and entourage
* You enchant a door, hole, cave, etc and pass through into the Goblin King's great hall.  A minister welcomes you, eager to arrange a matter for this esteemed guest.  The King himself is likely to get involved in interesting affairs.
	* Borrow a magic item from the royal vaults for a time.  With a good enough offer, buy it instead, or put in a commission with Weyland Smith.
	* Arrange a meeting with powerful being(s) (devils, djinn, faerie lords, dragons, dead souls, etc), here on neutral ground, safety guaranteed by the Goblin King.  He's good at goading people into showing up.  Couriers are ready to deliver messages and gifts across the cosmos.
	* Consult with the royal sages or spymasters.
	* Borrow a wing of the palace (along with servants and supplies) for a grand gala or private debauch.
	* Buy, sell, or wager intangibles, such as souls, beauty, luck, memories, noble lineage, or love, between yourself and another party, facilitated by the Goblin King.
