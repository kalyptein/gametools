### Invoking Glamours

* Activated through an act of will and an action, without gesture or incantation, except as noted.
	* Glamours don't require / allow a casting roll, and you can't Call Forth Wild Magic.
	* Using a Glamour doesn't limit movement and can't be disrupted.

Frequency
* Except as noted, Glamours can be used at-will.

### Invoking Runes

* Requires only an act of focus and will; no speech or gesturing, except as noted.
	* Using a Rune is an action, it limits movement and can be disrupted like a spell, but the Rune is not used up.
* Roll a CHA casting check (DC 11 for Minor, DC 13 for Major, and DC 15 for Mighty).
	* Normally only a natural 20 (crit) or natural 1 (botch) matters; otherwise the spell goes off normally, even on a "failure".  A botch create a faerie magic mishap (typically more weird than hazardous) and a 20 allows the usual doubling of a property, or a special effect.
		* If the mishap doesn't contradict the spell's normal results, they still happen.
	* Before the cast roll, you may decide to Call Forth Wild Magic.  Any success counts as a crit, and any failure counts as a botch.  You cannot spend Luck or Inspiration on this.

Usage by Level
* 1-4) Lesser (once a day), Greater (once a season), Mighty (once ever)
* 5-9)  Lesser (twice a day), Greater (once a week), Mighty (once a decade and a year)
* 10+) Lesser (thrice a day), Greater (once a day), Mighty (once a year and a day)

## List of Glamours

### Awe

- Usage: 1 / Turn
- Your dramatic gesture and otherworldly mien strike awe in a group of mortals and/or animals within 30', causing a morale check.
	- Affects creatures with a sum of Levels equal to your Level, starting with the lowest Level targets.
	- If the morale check fails, for 1d4 rounds, the targets are struck with your choice of fear (fleeing) or awe (offering respect and attention).
- If you speak a single person's name as you invoke, they have disadvantage on the morale check.

### Beguilement

- Usage: 1 / day per subject
- Your words have great power over a mortal you address within 30' (they must be able to understand you) who fails a save vs spells.
	- For 1d4 rounds, they regard your words as trustworthy.  You don't change their perceptions or memory, and you can't dictate how they react, given their belief.
	- You can't beguile them into directly harmful acts; they'll believe you, but for some reason they can't articulate, will hesitate to take the harmful action.
	- Afterward, it is usually immediately obvious that a magical deception has occurred, but if you haven't obvious lied or manipulated them, they may not realize what happened.

### Cloak of Darkness

- Usage: 1 / Turn
- You conjure a cloak of darkness (of voluminous but relatively normal size) and can drape it over yourself or another person or object (even covering a window, door, etc).
	- Attention slides away, leaving the covered subject invisible.
	- The cloak lasts as long as you concentrate and do not take action, after which it begins to fade.  If the subject moves or takes action, the cloak also begins to fade.  Once it begins fading, it lasts 1 round.
- Close illumination by a light source banishes the cloak (so you cannot be carrying a torch, lantern, candle, etc, and a searcher who comes right atop you with a light will reveal you).

### Coin Toss

- Usage: 1 / Turn
- You propose an immediate course of action, then flip a coin and left it fall to the ground.
	- Heads and tails indicate a likelihood of weal or woe within 10 minutes.  If it lands and rolls or wobbles before settling, there is some ambiguity, but one is stronger.  If there is true balance, the coin stand on edge; if neither is present, the coin rolls out of sight.  If the question is somehow invalid, the coin vanishes with a pop at the height of its flip.
- Reusing the glamour the same day in the same general area or about the same situation / action has a 3-in-6 chance of giving a random outcome instead.

### Defy the Depths

- Usage: 1 / Turn
- A creature or object (up to Large size) within 30' becomes buoyant in water.
	- The glamour lasts while you concentrate (able to move, but not take actions; taking damage or failing a save ends it).
	- Larger objects can be lightened (easier to raise by rope, help a ship stay afloat as you bail, etc), but won't fully float.
	- Used on yourself, you can walk carefully on water (mud, quicksand, snow) as if it were merely somewhat unfirm ground (+1x difficult terrain).

### Disguise Object

- Usage: at will
- You touch an object and place an illusion on it, making it seem like something different of roughly similar dimensions.
	- You can glamour up to (Level) object at a time, dismissing them at will.
	- The illusion is quite convincing: metal clinks, light reflects, the object seems of the right heft, etc.
	- If someone other than you touches the object (more than a brief brush), the illusion is broken, taking 1 round to fade.

### Fairy Dust

- Usage: 1 / day
- You conjure a small quantity of glittering dust into a pot, pouch, cupped hand, etc.
	- There is enough to fill a 10' radius if flung, or sprinkle over eight 5' squares.
	- It fades away after 1 day.
	- The dust is clingy and faintly luminous, but can only be seen by faeries and enchanters, unless a bit is sprinkled in the eyes first, allowing all such dust to be seen until the recipient next sleeps.
- Some possible uses: 
	- With a finger, the dust can be used to draw on things.
	- Dusted on a surface, it clings to hands or feet that touch it, leaving tracks.  It also grants +1 to reveal secret doors or similar subtle features, clinging to and limning them.
	- If it coats an invisible subject, the dust remains visible for 2d6 rounds before the concealing magic overcomes it, and it fades.

### Flame Charm

- Usage: at will
- You can conjure and extinguish small flames.
	- Flaming Fingertip: a colorful candle-flame appears on your fingertip, shedding 10' of light and able to light flammables.  It remains as long as you concentrate (you can move, but not take other actions).
	- Extinguish Flame: with a fingersnap, a small non-magical flame within 10', up to the size of a torch, is instantly extinguished.  If held by a creature, they may save vs spells to resist.
		- Instead of simply extinguishing, you may have the flame flare dazzlingly before going out.  Onlookers who fail a save vs spells are briefly blinded for a round (within 5' for a candle, up to 15' for a torch).

### Forgetting

- Usage: 1 / day per subject
- With a twinkle of your eye, a single mortal or animal within 30' must save vs Spells or forget the moment before (~1 round / ~10 seconds).

### Masquerade

- Usage: at will
- You alter the appearance of your head (face, hair, eyes, etc) with an illusion.
	- Minor changes take 1 round, dramatic ones take up to 1 Turn.
	- You cannot duplicate someone's appearance exactly; it never seems to work quite right.  At best you can look kind of like them.

### Mirth and Malice

- Usage: at will, but each person can only be affected once per day
- When speaking to a group of mortals within 30', you can impart a sense of good cheer, creeping discord, or worry and unease, either subtle or strong.
	- The subjects must be able to understand you, and you must speak for ~1 Turn (conversation, oratory, singing, etc).  Up to (Level) subjects must save vs spells.
	- Those who fail the save feel the emotion.  You don't directly control how they act on / interpret / direct their feelings, but the course of the interaction may guide it one way or another (talking about a topic while unease grows, etc).
	- When the interaction ends, the feelings persist for a further 1 Turn.

### Moon Sight

- Usage: always active
- The area around you, out to 60', is illuminated by an invisible full moon whose light only you can perceive.
	- The moonlight isn't bright enough to make out writing, unfamiliar faces, or other subtle details, but is enough to navigate by.
	- When you are in brighter illumination, the faint light is washed out, but your eyes adjust to it quickly in the low light or darkness.

### Savor

- Usage: once per day per subject
- With a flourishing gesture, pick a mortal or animal (by sight or name) and choose 1: 
	- Sprinkle faerie spice across a portion of food, causing it to smell and taste delicious to them.
	- Conjure their favorite treat into your hand (sweets, pastries, sizzling sausage, etc).  It carries one effect, if they eat it.
		- Their disposition toward you improves one step.
		- They become somewhat drowsy for a Turn (not a compulsion, but they may become distracted or nod off if there's no reason not to).
		- The treat tastes good for a moment, then become vile.
	- A conjured treat is real and nourishing for the subject, but small.  If a person eats nothing else, they can linger on, weak with hunger, but never actually starving to death.

### Seeming

* Usage: at will
* Your illusions can adjust the appearance of your clothing as you wish.
	* The glamour doesn't change it's properties (weight, warmth, etc) but it behaves completely realistically (sodden clothes don't seem to drip, though leave puddles where you were; cloaks flow in the breeze, etc).
	* A brief brush doesn't dispel the illusion, but a more solid touch does.

### Silver Tongue

- Usage: 1 / day
- Using echoes of the immortal tongue of Faerie, you gain the ability to speak (but not read or write) in a chosen language until you next sleep.
	- This can be a mortal or faerie language, or the language of a single kind of beast (felines, deer, cattle, canines, birds, etc), including monsters that share an aspect with that beast.

### Subtle Sight

- Usage: always active
- You can half-see invisible things (3-in-6 chance per creature / object).
	- If the DM forgets to check for it, and you remind them, you automatically succeed.
	- Each time an invisible subject attacks or otherwise interacts with you (including magic), you may check again.

### Through the Keyhole

- Usage: 1 / day per door/wall
- You peer closely at a keyhole, peephole, crack beneath a door, chink in a wall, narrow window, etc and teleport through to the other side.
	- You don't need to actually be able to see through, but the hole must actually go all the way through the surface, with room to appear (you can feel if it'll work).
	- You can pass through magically locked / reinforced doors and walls with a save vs Spells.  Magic that actually forbids teleportation blocks you.

### Vanishing

- Usage: 1 / day per target
- You disappear from the perceptions of a target within 60' for 1d3 rounds.  You possessions also vanish (reappearing if dropped), but light sources still illuminate the area.
	- If you attack or cast a spell on the target, you reappear.

### Ventriloquism

- Usage: at will
- You can cast your voice anywhere you can see, within 100'.  It can sound like any person or animal whose voice you've heard since you last woke up.

### Walk in Shadows

- Usage: at will
- You can search for the subtle doors that stand in shadow.
	- In a dark or dimly lit place, you can take a round to search a 10' x 10' area, which has a 2-in-6 chance of having a shadow door, which you can step through.
		- You can only go yourself (or with a familiar), with your carried possessions.  Multiple enchanters with this glamour can use the same door.
		- A door can only be used once, to enter or exit the halls of shadow.
		- After an hour, doors fade and form, so you can try to use doors in areas your already had, search areas again that lacked them, and doors you'd already found are lost.
	- Within the halls of shadow, you can roam.
		- Each round you stay, you have a 2-in-6 chance of finding a shadow door in a desired patch of dark within 100' of where you entered which you can emerge from.
		- If you haven't found a desired door yet, you may take a 3-in-6 chance to find a door to a random location within 100' (you'll find out where when you come out).
		- if you stay more than 10 rounds, the things that live in the halls start to hunt you (1-in-6 each round they find you).
		- If you are overburdened, reduce the chance-in-6 by 1 when navigating.
	- If you locate two doors on the earthly side before entering either, you can navigate from one to the other in a single round.
	- Things dropped in the halls either vanish forever, or find their way out into unexpected or strange places (particularly if they are powerful / magical / cursed / etc).
		- If you reetner before an hour has passed (from a door within 100' of where you last entered), you might be able to find a dropped thing (1-in-6 per round looking).

### Whistling Wind

- Usage: 1 / Turn
- You can command a brisk breeze to blow within 30' for 1d4 rounds.
	- It can weakly manipulate things (blow out candles, riffle pages, carry a sheet of parchment).
	- It can carry sounds from one place to another: whispers from a speaker to a listener, sweep sounds away from people you don't want to hear them, misdirect an observer as to the location of a sound, etc.

### Word Given

- Usage: once per day per person involved
- Parties (possibly including you) make a willing agreement and you invoke this glamour as you witness it.
	- If any party knowingly breaks the agreement, you and all participants know it, who did it, but not how.  If they broke it and only realize it later, the awareness occurs then.
	- If the deal is completed / obviated, you and all participants know it, and the glamour ends.
